## How to use this reference

Each entry has two parts:
- a **monograph** — mechanism, regulatory status, *human-evidence status with citations*, an honest evidence grade, reported-use patterns, rule-outs, and baseline labs; and
- a **▸ reference card** — the one-line quick sheet for the point of care.

**Classification key**
- 🟢 **Green** — FDA-approved with a clear clinical pathway (use per labeling + prescriber judgment).
- 🟡 **Yellow** — physician-review-only (off-label, investigational, or approved-abroad; heightened review).
- 🔴 **Red** — research-only / exclusion (unapproved; reported-use shown for *recognition*, not recommendation).

**Evidence grades:** **A** (multiple high-quality RCTs / approval) · **B** (≥1 solid RCT) · **C** (limited/early human data) · **D** (preclinical or thin/absent human evidence).

# I. REPAIR AXIS

## 1. BPC-157 · 🔴 not approved · Grade D
**Mechanism:** Synthetic gastric pentadecapeptide; reported pro-angiogenic + nitric-oxide modulation, soft-tissue/GI healing (mechanism almost entirely preclinical).
**Evidence:** No completed published human efficacy RCT. One Phase 1 safety/PK (NCT02637284, status unknown); one Phase 2 *recruiting* (NCT07437547, hamstring strain). FDA barred it from compounding (2023).
- Gwyer 2019, *Cell Tissue Res* — https://doi.org/10.1007/s00441-019-03016-8 · Seiwerth 2021, *Front Pharmacol* — https://doi.org/10.3389/fphar.2021.627533 · Sikiric 2020, *Curr Pharm Des* — https://doi.org/10.2174/1381612826666200424180139
**Reported-use** *(marketplace):* 5 mg vial + 2.5 mL BAC = 2 mg/mL; reported 250–500 mcg SC 1–2×/day (often near injury); 2–6 wk cycles.
**Rule out:** active/prior malignancy (pro-angiogenic), pregnancy, lactation, age <18, hypersensitivity, unverified source. WADA-prohibited.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, fasting glucose; cancer screen current.
> **▸ Card —** BPC-157 · *reported* 250–500 mcg SC 1–2×/day, 2–6 wk · rule out malignancy/pregnancy/<18 · labs CBC, CMP, glucose · no completed human efficacy data (1 Ph2 recruiting) · reference — provider signs

## 2. TB-500 / Thymosin β4 · 🔴 not approved · Grade D (consumer fragment)
**Mechanism:** Tβ4 is a G-actin-sequestering peptide (cell migration, angiogenesis, healing); "TB-500" is the synthetic Ac-LKKTETQ fragment sold to consumers.
**Evidence:** Full-length Tβ4 (RGN-259) has completed Phase 3 ophthalmic RCTs; the **consumer fragment has no completed human efficacy trial** (NCT07487363, Ph1/2, recruiting). PubMed coverage of literal "TB-500" is mostly doping analytics.
- Esposito 2012, *Drug Test Anal* — https://doi.org/10.1002/dta.1402 · Ho 2012, *J Chromatogr A* — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chroma.2012.09.043
**Reported-use** *(marketplace):* 5 mg + 2 mL BAC = 2.5 mg/mL; reported 2–2.5 mg SC twice weekly ×4–6 wk loading, then ~2 mg q1–2 wk.
**Rule out:** active/prior malignancy, pregnancy, lactation, age <18, hypersensitivity, unverified source. WADA-prohibited.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, glucose; cardiovascular history.
> **▸ Card —** TB-500 · *reported* 2–2.5 mg SC 2×/wk ×4–6 wk, then maintenance · rule out malignancy/pregnancy · labs CBC, CMP · consumer fragment has no completed human trial · reference — provider signs

## 3. GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide-1) · 🟡/🔴 cosmetic ingredient · Grade C topical / D systemic
**Mechanism:** Copper-binding tripeptide; reported collagen/elastin stimulation, wound-healing gene modulation (skin).
**Evidence:** Mostly topical/cosmetic + in-vitro. A small topical RCT post-laser showed improved satisfaction but **no objective benefit**. Ph2 topical RCT recruiting (NCT07437586). No completed RCT for injected/systemic use.
- Miller 2006, *Arch Facial Plast Surg* — https://doi.org/10.1001/archfaci.8.4.252 · Dymek 2023, *Pharmaceutics* — https://doi.org/10.3390/pharmaceutics15102485
**Reported-use** *(marketplace):* topical 1–2% serum (best-supported); injectable (gray-market) 50 mg + 5 mL BAC = 10 mg/mL, reported 1–2 mg/day SC — unapproved/poorly studied.
**Rule out:** copper sensitivity / Wilson disease (systemic), pregnancy/lactation for systemic, hypersensitivity.
**Labs:** systemic only — serum copper, ceruloplasmin, CMP. Topical needs none.
> **▸ Card —** GHK-Cu · topical 1–2% (best evidence); *reported* injectable 1–2 mg/day SC (unproven) · rule out copper overload/Wilson · labs (systemic) copper, ceruloplasmin · objective benefit unproven even topically · reference — provider signs

# II. METABOLISM AXIS

## 4. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound) · 🟢 FDA-approved · Grade A
**Mechanism:** Dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist — insulin secretion, appetite suppression, weight loss.
**Evidence:** Extensive completed Phase 3 program (SURMOUNT-1: up to ~−20.9% body weight at 72 wk; SURPASS, SUMMIT, OSA). FDA-approved for T2D, chronic weight management, OSA in obesity.
- Jastreboff 2022, *NEJM* (SURMOUNT-1) — https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2206038
**Use** *(label):* pens 2.5–15 mg. Start 2.5 mg SC weekly ×4 wk; titrate +2.5 mg q≥4 wk to max 15 mg. SC.
**Rule out:** personal/family MTC or MEN2 (boxed), pancreatitis history, gallbladder disease, severe gastroparesis; caution: reduced oral-contraceptive efficacy, hypoglycemia with insulin/sulfonylureas.
**Labs:** A1c, fasting glucose, CMP, lipids, weight/BMI; pregnancy status; lipase if pancreatitis risk.
> **▸ Card —** Tirzepatide · 2.5 mg SC weekly ×4 → titrate +2.5 q4wk → max 15 mg · rule out MTC/MEN2/pancreatitis · labs A1c, CMP, lipids · **Grade A, FDA-approved** · on-label use per prescriber judgment

## 5. Retatrutide · 🟡 investigational (Ph3 ongoing) · Grade B
**Mechanism:** Triple GIP/GLP-1/glucagon receptor agonist — weight loss, glycemic control, hepatic fat reduction.
**Evidence:** Completed Phase 2 obesity RCT (~−24.2% body weight at 48 wk); Phase 3 (TRIUMPH-1 NCT05929066 completed; NCT05882045 active). Not yet approved.
- Jastreboff/Kaplan/Frías 2023, *NEJM* — https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2301972 · Katsi 2025, *Biomolecules* — https://doi.org/10.3390/biom15060796
**Reported-use** *(trial dosing; no commercial product):* SC once weekly, escalated 2 mg → 8–12 mg. Any consumer "retatrutide" is unapproved/unverified.
**Rule out:** incretin-class cautions (MTC/MEN2, pancreatitis), dose-dependent heart-rate rise; investigational — full safety unknown.
**Labs:** A1c, glucose, CMP, lipids, resting HR/BP; pregnancy status.
> **▸ Card —** Retatrutide · *trial* SC weekly 2 mg→8–12 mg · rule out MTC/MEN2/pancreatitis, watch HR · labs A1c, CMP, HR/BP · investigational, strong Ph2, not approved · reference — provider signs

## 6. AOD-9604 · 🔴 not approved (development halted) · Grade D
**Mechanism:** hGH 177–191 lipolytic C-terminal fragment; reported lipolysis without IGF-1/glucose effects (mainly mouse data).
**Evidence:** Phase 2 obesity development **discontinued for lack of meaningful efficacy**; **zero registered trials**; substantive data preclinical.
- Heffernan 2001, *Endocrinology* (preclinical) — https://doi.org/10.1210/endo.142.12.8522 · Wilding 2004, *Curr Opin Investig Drugs* (review, PMID 15134286)
**Reported-use** *(marketplace):* 5 mg + 2 mL BAC = 2.5 mg/mL; reported ~300 mcg/day SC (fasted/AM); ~12-wk cycles.
**Rule out:** unapproved, no demonstrated efficacy, no robust safety database, pregnancy/lactation. WADA-prohibited.
**Labs:** fasting glucose, A1c, lipids, CMP.
> **▸ Card —** AOD-9604 · *reported* 300 mcg/day SC, ~12 wk · rule out pregnancy/<18 · labs glucose, lipids · **human obesity development was halted for lack of efficacy** · reference — provider signs

# III. GROWTH AXIS

## 7. Sermorelin (GHRH 1-29) · 🟡 off-label (formerly approved, discontinued) · Grade B (peds/dx) / D (adult wellness)
**Mechanism:** N-terminal GHRH fragment; stimulates endogenous pulsatile GH via the pituitary GHRH receptor.
**Evidence:** Real FDA history — was approved (Geref) for pediatric GHD diagnosis/treatment, now discontinued; compounded use not FDA-approved. No RCTs for adult "anti-aging" use.
- Prakash & Goa 1999, *BioDrugs* — https://doi.org/10.2165/00063030-199912020-00007 · Walker 2006, *Clin Interv Aging* — https://doi.org/10.2147/ciia.2006.1.4.307 · Esposito 2003, *Adv Drug Deliv Rev* — https://doi.org/10.1016/s0169-409x(03)00109-1
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; verified peds label was 30 mcg/kg/day):* 5–15 mg vial; reported 200–500 mcg SC at bedtime, nightly, months-long cycles.
**Rule out:** active malignancy, hypersensitivity; untreated hypothyroidism blunts response (correct first); caution in hyperglycemia/obesity.
**Labs:** IGF-1, TSH/free T4 ← gating, fasting glucose/A1c; exclude occult malignancy.
> **▸ Card —** Sermorelin · *reported* 200–500 mcg SC nightly · rule out malignancy, treat thyroid first · labs IGF-1, TSH, glucose · adult anti-aging use has no RCTs; brand discontinued · reference — provider signs

## 8. Ipamorelin · 🔴 not approved · Grade C/D
**Mechanism:** Selective ghrelin-receptor (GHS-R1a) agonist / GH secretagogue; minimal cortisol/prolactin effect.
**Evidence:** Two completed human Phase 2 trials (GI motility) — **both negative** (NCT00672074 missed primary endpoint; NCT01280344). Zero human efficacy data for anti-aging/muscle/fat use.
- Raun 1998, *Eur J Endocrinol* — https://doi.org/10.1530/eje.0.1390552 · Beck 2014, *Int J Colorectal Dis* (negative human Ph2) — https://doi.org/10.1007/s00384-014-2030-8
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; one human trial used IV 0.03 mg/kg BID):* 5–10 mg vial; reported 200–300 mcg SC 1–3×/day, often stacked with a GHRH analog.
**Rule out:** active malignancy, hypersensitivity; theoretical appetite/glucose effects.
**Labs:** IGF-1, TSH/free T4, fasting glucose/A1c.
> **▸ Card —** Ipamorelin · *reported* 200–300 mcg SC nightly · rule out malignancy, treat thyroid first · labs IGF-1, TSH, glucose · **only human efficacy trials (GI) FAILED** · reference — provider signs

## 9. CJC-1295 · 🔴 not approved · Grade C (DAC) / D (no-DAC)
**Mechanism:** Long-acting GHRH analog. *With-DAC* binds albumin → multi-day half-life (the form in the human RCT); *"no-DAC"/mod-GRF 1-29* is short-acting with no dedicated human trials.
**Evidence:** One human PK/PD RCT of the DAC form (dose-dependent GH/IGF-1 rise, t½ ~6–8 days); Phase 2 NCT00267527 terminated. "No-DAC" borrows the DAC molecule's credibility.
- Teichman 2006, *JCEM* (human DAC RCT) — https://doi.org/10.1210/jc.2005-1536 · Ionescu & Frohman 2006, *JCEM* — https://doi.org/10.1210/jc.2006-1702 · Jetté 2005, *Endocrinology* (rat) — https://doi.org/10.1210/en.2004-1286
**Reported-use** *(marketplace):* no-DAC ~2 mg vial, 100 mcg SC 1–3×/day; DAC ~1–2 mg SC weekly.
**Rule out:** active malignancy, hypersensitivity, pregnancy; sustained high IGF-1 (esp. DAC) → edema/arthralgia/insulin-resistance/neoplasia concerns.
**Labs:** IGF-1 (monitor), TSH/free T4, fasting glucose/A1c.
> **▸ Card —** CJC-1295 · *reported* no-DAC 100 mcg SC 1–3×/day; DAC 1–2 mg weekly · rule out malignancy · labs IGF-1, TSH · **only the DAC form has any human data** · reference — provider signs

## 10. CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin (blend) · 🔴 not approved · Grade D
**Mechanism (rationale):** GHRH analog + ghrelin agonist for a putatively synergistic GH pulse.
**Evidence:** **No human trials of the combination — period.** Supporting data are animal + review-level only (a 2026 sports-medicine primer states evidence is "limited to animal studies").
- Mayfield 2026, *Am J Sports Med* — https://doi.org/10.1177/03635465251357593 · Coutinho 2026, *J Sports Med Phys Fitness* — https://doi.org/10.23736/S0022-4707.26.17773-1 · Mavrych 2026, *Front Aging* — https://doi.org/10.3389/fragi.2026.1790247
**Reported-use** *(marketplace blend):* "CJC no-DAC 2 mg + ipamorelin 2 mg" vial; reported ~100/100 mcg SC at bedtime, 5-on/2-off, 8–12 wk.
**Rule out:** union of components — active malignancy, hypersensitivity, pregnancy; additive uncertainty.
**Labs:** IGF-1, TSH/free T4, fasting glucose/A1c.
> **▸ Card —** CJC+Ipamorelin · *reported* 100/100 mcg SC nightly · **the popular stack has ZERO human combination trials** · rule out malignancy, treat thyroid first · labs IGF-1, TSH · reference — provider signs

## 11. Tesamorelin (Egrifta) · 🟢 FDA-approved · Grade A (label indication)
**Mechanism:** Stabilized GHRH(1-44) analog; restores pulsatile GH, raises IGF-1, selectively reduces visceral adipose tissue.
**Evidence:** FDA-approved (2010) for HIV-associated lipodystrophy. Two pivotal Phase 3 RCTs (pooled VAT −15.4% vs placebo, glucose-neutral) + ongoing MASLD/liver trials. Off-label for general "anti-aging."
- Falutz 2010, *JCEM* (pivotal Ph3 pooled) — https://doi.org/10.1210/jc.2010-0490 · Fourman 2020, *JCI Insight* — https://doi.org/10.1172/jci.insight.140134 · Dhillon 2011, *Drugs* — https://doi.org/10.2165/11202240-000000000-00000
**Use** *(verified label):* 2 mg SC once daily (abdomen); reconstituted lyophilized powder (Egrifta SV simplifies); VAT re-accumulates on discontinuation.
**Rule out:** pregnancy, active malignancy, disrupted hypothalamic-pituitary axis (tumor/surgery/radiation), hypersensitivity; monitor glucose, edema, arthralgia.
**Labs:** IGF-1 (baseline + monitor) + TSH/free T4 ← gating, fasting glucose/A1c, lipids; exclude malignancy + pituitary pathology.
> **▸ Card —** Tesamorelin · **2 mg SC daily** (label) · rule out pregnancy/malignancy/pituitary disease · labs IGF-1, TSH, glucose · **Grade A, FDA-approved (HIV lipodystrophy); other uses off-label** · per prescriber judgment

# IV. LONGEVITY AXIS

## 12. MOTS-c · 🔴 not approved · Grade D→C
**Mechanism:** Mitochondrial-derived 16-aa peptide; AMPK activation, metabolic flexibility ("exercise mimetic").
**Evidence:** Preclinical-dominant; human data observational/associational. First interventional **Phase 2a RCT now recruiting** (NCT07505745, prediabetes/obesity, n=120). No completed human efficacy trials.
- Lee 2015, *Cell Metab* — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cmet.2015.02.009 · Kumagai 2021, *Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab* — https://doi.org/10.1152/ajpendo.00275.2020 · Zheng 2023, *Front Endocrinol* — https://doi.org/10.3389/fendo.2023.1120533
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; no validated regimen):* 10 mg + 2 mL BAC = 5 mg/mL; reported 5–10 mg SC 2–3×/wk, 4–8 wk blocks.
**Rule out:** active malignancy (precaution), pregnancy/lactation, age <18, hypersensitivity.
**Labs:** fasting glucose + insulin (HOMA-IR), A1c, CMP, lipids, CBC; cancer screen.
> **▸ Card —** MOTS-c · *reported* 5–10 mg SC 2–3×/wk, 4–8 wk · rule out malignancy/pregnancy · labs HOMA-IR, A1c · preclinical-dominant; Ph2a recruiting · reference — provider signs

## 13. Elamipretide / SS-31 · 🟡 investigational · Grade B−
**Mechanism:** Cardiolipin-binding tetrapeptide; stabilizes inner mitochondrial membrane, improves ATP, reduces ROS.
**Evidence:** 21 registered trials, but the **pivotal Phase 3 in mitochondrial myopathy (MMPOWER-3) missed endpoints and was terminated**; AMD/Barth ongoing; a Ph2a aging trial recruiting (NCT07275424). No approved indication.
- Karaa 2023, *Neurology* (MMPOWER-3) — https://doi.org/10.1212/WNL.0000000000207402 · Karaa 2020, *J Cachexia Sarcopenia Muscle* — https://doi.org/10.1002/jcsm.12559 · Szeto 2014, *Br J Pharmacol* — https://doi.org/10.1111/bph.12461
**Reported-use** *(trials used 40 mg/day SC):* ~50 mg vial + 2 mL BAC = 25 mg/mL; reported 5–40 mg SC once daily, weeks–months.
**Rule out:** pregnancy/lactation; injection-site reactions very common (~80%); unknown long-term safety.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, CK, fasting glucose/A1c.
> **▸ Card —** Elamipretide/SS-31 · *trials* 40 mg/day SC · rule out pregnancy · labs CBC, CMP, CK · **best-studied longevity peptide — but pivotal Phase 3 FAILED; not approved** · reference — provider signs

## 14. Epitalon (AEDG) · 🔴 not approved · Grade D
**Mechanism:** Synthetic pineal tetrapeptide; reported telomerase induction (in vitro), melatonin-rhythm normalization.
**Evidence:** **Zero registered trials.** Telomere/longevity claims rest on in-vitro + animal + weak older Russian reports. No Western replication.
- Khavinson 2003, *Bull Exp Biol Med* (in vitro) — https://doi.org/10.1023/a:1025493705728 · Anisimov 2002, *Cancer Lett* (rat) — https://doi.org/10.1016/s0304-3835(02)00090-3 · Khavinson 2002, *Neuro Endocrinol Lett* (PMID 12374906)
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; no validated dosing):* 10 mg + 2 mL BAC = 5 mg/mL; reported 5–10 mg SC daily, 10–20 day cycles 1–2×/yr.
**Rule out:** active malignancy (telomerase theory — animal data are anti-carcinogenic; net human effect unknown), pregnancy/lactation; unknown safety.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, glucose; age-appropriate cancer screening.
> **▸ Card —** Epitalon · *reported* 5–10 mg SC daily, 10–20 day pulses · **zero human trials; telomere claims are in-vitro/animal** · rule out malignancy · cancer screen · reference — provider signs

# V. COGNITION AXIS

## 15. Cerebrolysin · 🟡 not US-approved (approved abroad) · Grade B/C
**Mechanism:** Porcine brain-derived peptide/amino-acid mix; reported neurotrophic/neuroprotective activity.
**Evidence:** 42 trials + meta-analyses, **mixed**: favorable Alzheimer's meta-analysis + positive post-stroke RCT (CARS), but a **stroke meta-analysis found no significant efficacy.** Approved in several non-US countries.
- Muresanu 2016, *Stroke* (CARS) — https://doi.org/10.1161/STROKEAHA.115.009416 · Gauthier 2015, *Dement Geriatr Cogn Disord* (AD meta) — https://doi.org/10.1159/000377672 · Zhang 2017, *Biomed Res Int* (negative stroke meta) — https://doi.org/10.1155/2017/4191670
**Use** *(clinical abroad — IV/IM, NOT a reconstituted SC powder):* ampoules/solution; trials used 30 mL/day IV ×10–21 days.
**Rule out:** hypersensitivity; epilepsy/status epilepticus (caution); severe renal impairment; porcine-derived (allergy + religious/dietary); anaphylactoid reactions reported.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, allergy + seizure history; neuroimaging in stroke/AD context.
> **▸ Card —** Cerebrolysin · 30 mL/day IV/IM ×10–21 days (not SC) · rule out epilepsy/renal/porcine allergy · **mixed evidence; approved abroad ≠ proven** · per prescriber judgment

## 16. Semax (ACTH 4-10 analog) · 🔴 not approved (Russia-marketed) · Grade C/D
**Mechanism:** ACTH(4-10) heptapeptide analog; raises BDNF, modulates dopamine/serotonin; reported nootropic/neuroprotective.
**Evidence:** **Zero genuine registered trials.** Best human data = a small non-randomized Russian acute-stroke study; rest preclinical. Marketed intranasally in Russia only.
- Gusev 1997, *Zh Nevrol Psikhiatr* (PMID 11517472) · Dolotov 2006, *J Neurochem* (rat) — https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1471-4159.2006.03658.x
**Reported-use** *(matches Russian intranasal product):* nasal drops 0.1%/1%; the 1997 study used 12–18 mg/day ×5–10 days.
**Rule out:** limited safety data; pregnancy/lactation; nasal irritation.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, glucose.
> **▸ Card —** Semax · intranasal, *reported* ~12–18 mg/day (Russian study) · **no registered trials; small non-randomized Russian data** · rule out pregnancy · reference — provider signs

## 17. Selank (tuftsin analog) · 🔴 not approved (Russia-marketed) · Grade C/D
**Mechanism:** Tuftsin analog; anxiolytic via inhibition of enkephalin degradation + monoaminergic/GABA modulation.
**Evidence:** **Zero genuine registered trials.** Two small Russian comparative anxiety trials (vs benzodiazepines) + preclinical. Marketed intranasally in Russia only.
- Zozulia 2008, *Zh Nevrol Psikhiatr* (PMID 18454096) · Medvedev 2014, *Zh Nevrol Psikhiatr* (PMID 25176261) · Kasian 2017, *Behav Neurol* (rat) — https://doi.org/10.1155/2017/5091027
**Reported-use** *(matches Russian intranasal "Selank 0.15%"):* nasal drops 0.15%; reported ~250–500 mcg/day intranasal, ~2 wk cycles.
**Rule out:** limited safety data; pregnancy/lactation; not a substitute for evaluating a diagnosed anxiety disorder.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP.
> **▸ Card —** Selank · intranasal *reported* 250–500 mcg/day · **2 small Russian anxiety trials; no registered/Western trials** · rule out pregnancy · reference — provider signs

## 18. DSIP (delta sleep-inducing peptide) · 🔴 not approved · Grade D
**Mechanism:** Nonapeptide (isolated 1977); proposed sleep/stress modulation — but its role and the sleep link remain unresolved.
**Evidence:** **Zero registered trials.** Old, sparse, inconclusive human data; reviewers note DSIP does not reliably promote sleep. Modern data preclinical.
- Graf & Kastin 1986, *Peptides* — https://doi.org/10.1016/0196-9781(86)90148-8 · Kovalzon & Strekalova 2006, *J Neurochem* — https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1471-4159.2006.03693.x
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; not validated):* 5 mg + 2 mL BAC = 2.5 mg/mL; reported ~100–200 mcg SC at night.
**Rule out:** unknown human safety; pregnancy/lactation; address sleep hygiene/OSA first.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP; sleep evaluation.
> **▸ Card —** DSIP · *reported* 100–200 mcg SC at night · **50-yr-old peptide whose sleep effect was never confirmed; no trials** · rule out OSA first · reference — provider signs

# VI. IMMUNITY AXIS

## 19. Thymosin α1 (thymalfasin; Zadaxin) · 🟡 not US-approved (approved abroad) · Grade B–C
**Mechanism:** Synthetic 28-aa thymic peptide; immunomodulator augmenting T-cell function and innate immunity.
**Evidence:** Real RCTs — chronic hepatitis B (NCT00291616, NCT02366208), melanoma adjuvant (NCT00911443), pancreatitis (TRACE), colorectal adjuvant (NCT05086614 recruiting). Approved as Zadaxin abroad; not FDA-approved. Robust for *specific* indications, **not** "vitality/anti-aging."
- Ancell 2001, *Am J Health Syst Pharm* — https://doi.org/10.1093/ajhp/58.10.886 · Zhou 2020, *BMJ Open* — https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjopen-2020-037231
**Reported-use** *(marketplace):* 1.6 mg or 5 mg vial + ~1 mL diluent; reported 1.6 mg SC twice weekly (hepatitis dosing) up to 1.6 mg daily.
**Rule out:** hypersensitivity; caution in active autoimmune disease and transplant/immunosuppressed; pregnancy/lactation.
**Labs:** CBC w/ diff, CMP/LFTs, hepatitis B/C serologies if relevant, ANA if indicated.
> **▸ Card —** Thymosin α1 · *reported* 1.6 mg SC 2×/wk · rule out autoimmune/transplant-immunosuppression · labs CBC, LFTs · **RCT evidence is indication-specific, not "vitality"; not FDA-approved** · reference — provider signs

## 20. KPV (lysine-proline-valine; α-MSH 11-13) · 🔴 not approved · Grade D
**Mechanism:** C-terminal α-MSH tripeptide; anti-inflammatory via NF-κB/TNF-α downregulation; colonic uptake via PepT1.
**Evidence:** **Preclinical only — zero human trials of any phase** (mouse colitis/IBD + in-vitro).
- Kannengiesser 2008, *Inflamm Bowel Dis* — https://doi.org/10.1002/ibd.20334 · Viennois 2016, *Cell Mol Gastroenterol Hepatol* — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jcmgh.2016.01.006 · Xiao 2017, *Mol Ther* — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ymthe.2016.11.020
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; no validated human dosing):* ~5–10 mg vial; reported ~200–500 mcg/day oral/topical/SC — anecdotal.
**Rule out:** no human safety data (the principal caution); pregnancy/lactation; hypersensitivity; sourcing/purity risk.
**Labs:** CBC, CMP, hs-CRP (to track the inflammatory rationale).
> **▸ Card —** KPV · *reported* 200–500 mcg/day (anecdotal) · **no human trials of any kind — say so to the patient** · labs CBC, CMP, hs-CRP · reference — provider signs

# VII. VITALITY AXIS

## 21. PT-141 / Bremelanotide (Vyleesi) · 🟢 FDA-approved · Grade A (label indication)
**Mechanism:** Cyclic α-MSH analog; central melanocortin (MC4R) agonist increasing sexual desire.
**Evidence:** FDA-approved (2019) for acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women. Two completed Phase 3 RCTs (RECONNECT) + Phase 2; effect modest. Grade A *for the label indication only*; male/general "libido" use is off-label.
- Kingsberg 2019, *Obstet Gynecol* (RECONNECT) — https://doi.org/10.1097/AOG.0000000000003500 · Clayton 2016, *Womens Health* — https://doi.org/10.2217/whe-2016-0018 · Diamond 2006, *J Sex Med* — https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1743-6109.2006.00268.x
**Use** *(label):* Vyleesi 1.75 mg/0.3 mL autoinjector SC PRN ~45 min before activity; max 1 dose/24 h, ≤8/month.
**Rule out (KEY — cardiovascular):** uncontrolled hypertension or known CVD — **contraindicated** (transient BP rise/HR drop). AEs: nausea (often significant), flushing, headache, focal hyperpigmentation with repeat use. Not for postmenopausal women or men per label.
**Labs/assessment:** baseline BP + CV risk assessment (essential); rule out reversible causes of low desire; pregnancy status.
> **▸ Card —** Bremelanotide/PT-141 · Vyleesi 1.75 mg SC PRN ≤8/mo · **rule out uncontrolled HTN/CVD (contraindicated)** · check baseline BP · **Grade A for premenopausal HSDD only; male use off-label** · per prescriber judgment

## 22. Kisspeptin-10 · 🟡 investigational · Grade C
**Mechanism:** KISS1-derived peptide; GPR54/KISS1R agonist on hypothalamic GnRH neurons → GnRH → LH/FSH → gonadal steroidogenesis.
**Evidence:** Genuine human Phase 1 physiology studies (reliably stimulates LH acutely) — NCT00914823, NCT03286517 — but **no completed efficacy RCTs** for libido/fertility/"vitality."
- Ullah 2018, *Andrologia* — https://doi.org/10.1111/and.13219 · Lippincott 2016, *JCEM* — https://doi.org/10.1210/jc.2016-1545 · Colledge 2008, *Results Probl Cell Differ* — https://doi.org/10.1007/400_2007_050
**Reported-use** *(marketplace; not validated therapeutic dosing):* ~5–10 mg vial. Research human dosing ≈ 1 µg/kg IV (≠ marketplace SC microdose). Half-life minutes → transient effects only; durable benefit not established.
**Rule out:** hormone-sensitive conditions, reproductive cancers (theoretical), pregnancy; no long-term human safety data.
**Labs:** LH, FSH, total testosterone (estradiol in women), prolactin; consider SHBG.
> **▸ Card —** Kisspeptin-10 · *reported* SC microdose (research dosing was ~1 µg/kg IV) · short half-life → transient only · rule out hormone-sensitive cancer/pregnancy · labs LH, FSH, testosterone · **acute hormonal effect proven; therapeutic benefit not** · reference — provider signs
