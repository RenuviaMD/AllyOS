Can I add Vitamin C to chair 1? The patient is G6PD-deficient.
Grade A · safety
🔴 No — hard stop. High-dose IV Vitamin C can trigger acute hemolysis in G6PD deficiency. Avoid > 2 g; the bench will block it. If a low dose is clinically intended, that's the prescriber's call to document.
Is Niagen the same as IV NAD+?
No. Niagen / NR is an NAD+ precursor — a different molecule from infused NAD+, with no proven peptide synergy. Evidence grade C–D (small studies). Needs a prescriber-signed standing order. VERIFY before relying on it.
