EMR UI/UX — What the Best Do, and the PI Master UI v2 Blueprint

Research synthesis (KLAS usability leaders, click-burden evidence, chart-layout conventions, PI/chiro market) → concrete improvements on top of the April design you like. July 2026.

1 · What the usability leaders in your segment actually do

Who wins on usability (KLAS 2025–2026): Elation Health (small-practice ambulatory, two years running), athenahealth (independent practices), Canvas Medical (ambulatory specialty). In the PI/chiro-adjacent world, ChiroTouch dominates with one headline claim: a SOAP note in ~15 seconds via macros.

Their shared DNA — six patterns:

  1. Schedule-first home. The workday starts on a "Today's patients" queue, not a blank form. One tap opens the patient's encounter.
  2. Persistent patient banner. Once a chart is open, a colored banner pins the patient's identity (name, age/sex, DOB, carrier, status) across every screen — you never lose context.
  3. Chronological record + timeline. The chart reads newest-first with action items pinned on top; a timeline jumps to any date.
  4. Click economy as a religion. Documented driver of burnout: click counts and screen-hopping. Leaders count clicks-per-task and design "defaults-first, document by exception."
  5. Macros / quick text. One tap inserts standard findings; templates per visit type. This is how "15-second SOAP" happens.
  6. AI scribe is now table stakes (2026). Every major EHR shipped or expanded one in 2025. Structured input → AI-drafted narrative → physician review. PI Master already has this — ahead of the segment.

2 · The blueprint: your April design + eight targeted upgrades

Legend: OLD BUILD already in your April design (keep) · ENGINE already in the new platform (keep) · ADD the improvement.

U1 — Today's Visits as the landing screen ADD

Per-role home: staff see today's check-ins and packet status; the physician sees the queue with one-tap "Open encounter"; PT sees today's sessions. The registry and search (from the old sidebar OLD BUILD) sit one click away. This is the single biggest minutes-per-day win.

U2 — Persistent patient banner ADD

Once a patient is open: FALCON, Armando · 60M · DOB 05/07/1966 · Allstate · Claim # — · DOA 06/20/2026 (day 14) · INITIAL pinned at the top of every section, with the case-status badge vocabulary from the old build OLD BUILD. The "days post-accident" counter is PI-specific gold: it makes the 14-day window visible at a glance.

U3 — Encounter stepper instead of one long scroll ADD

The old MD Visit module was a full-screen phased encounter OLD BUILD — restore that: one phase per screen (History → Exam → Assessment → Plan → Sign), progress dots, Enter-to-advance. The audit chip ENGINE becomes the gate on the Sign step. Staff intake keeps its own 2-step flow.

U4 — Defaults-first exam with macros ADD

Extend the existing "mark region WNL" buttons ENGINE to the ChiroTouch pattern: whole-exam-normal in one tap, then document only exceptions; a physician phrase library (your own quick-texts) for HPI notes and procedure notes. Target: routine follow-up documented in under 60 seconds.

U5 — Case timeline in Case Detail ADD

The old Case Detail overlay OLD BUILD gains a chronological record fed by the engine's archive ENGINE: every visit, document, order, and superbill newest-first; pending items (unsigned packet forms, EMC pending, imaging not reviewed) pinned on top as action chips.

U6 — Patient search everywhere OLD BUILD

The old sidebar's 3-characters search (last name, first, or phone digits) returns, wired to the engine's multi-clinic data ENGINE. Keyboard shortcut: "/" focuses search from anywhere.

U7 — AI as a first-class encounter action ENGINE ADD

The AI Initial Report and HPI draft move from toolbar buttons into the encounter flow itself — the Plan/Sign step offers "Draft report" inline with review. The market made AI scribing table stakes; yours is spec-locked and PHI-minimized, which none of the off-the-shelf products can claim for FL PIP.

U8 — Visual system: keep your identity, tune for hours of use OLD BUILD ADD

Keep the dark canvas + teal/gold brand. Tune per the eye-fatigue evidence: slightly lower contrast surfaces, one accent per screen, consistent status colors (the old badge vocabulary), 15–20% larger touch targets for tablet use at the front desk, and soft section transitions instead of hard borders.

3 · What NOT to copy from big EMRs

  • Mega-navigation (Epic-style 40-item menus) — your product wins by having five destinations, not forty.
  • Alert storms — keep the single audit chip; never stack popups.
  • Configurable-everything — one opinionated FL-PIP workflow is the product; configurability is how big EMRs became unusable.
