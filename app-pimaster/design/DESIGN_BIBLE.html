0What PI Master Is, and What This Repository Contains

PI Master™ is a Florida Personal Injury Protection (PIP) clinical documentation system for a single-physician PI clinic. Its core thesis, stated on its own login screen: “PI Master™ makes PI documentation faster, cleaner, and denial-proof.” Every design decision serves that thesis: structured binary inputs instead of free text, automatic narrative generation, enforced medical-necessity language, statutory signature choreography, and billing packages that are pre-audited against the CMS-1500 standard before submission.

The one-sentence operating model

Staff screens and checks in the patient (14-day rule enforced at the front desk) and collects a 6-form signed reception packet → the physician completes a pill-and-checkbox structured encounter → the system generates the narrative report, signs it with the physician’s embedded credentials, emails imaging orders to MAZEL Diagnostic, and notifies staff → staff prints, obtains the patient’s statutory counter-signature (Disclosure Form or Patient Log), and files the insurance package → PT documents daily sessions and weekly summaries that flow back into the physician’s follow-up view → at MMI the case is discharged, closed, and the complete package is submitted.

1.4 The five design principles that make it work

  1. Binary in, narrative out. The physician never composes prose. Pills, pain scales, and checklists feed deterministic note generators that emit defensible, consistent, PIP-compliant narrative. (Spec: “All binary inputs — AI generates narrative.”)
  2. Console vs. paper. Dark UI = workspace; white Georgia-serif = anything an insurer, attorney, or patient will read. Users always know when they are looking at a legal artifact.
  3. Color is clinical semantics. Pain, signature state, EMC status, batch status, ROM grade, and discipline (MD/PT/staff) each own a hue. Nothing is decorated; everything is coded.
  4. Compliance rendered as UI. The 14-day countdown is a colored banner; Modifier 25 is auto-applied and explained inline; the CMS-1500 audit shows hard stops in red before a package can be built; unsigned notes block claims.
  5. Deterministic autopopulation with physician override. ROM findings preselect ICD-10; complaints preselect imaging orders with written justification; modalities derive CPT codes — and every auto-selection remains editable and visibly labeled (“Indicated”, “Auto-populated”).

5.3 Florida PIP & regulatory vocabulary

PIP
Personal Injury Protection — Florida no-fault auto medical coverage. Pays 80% of medical bills and 60% of lost wages, up to the benefit limit.
14-Day Rule
Initial treatment must occur within 14 calendar days of the accident or the PIP claim is denied entirely (FL §627.736). Clinic policy: staff screens at admission; >14 days is not accepted.
EMC — Emergency Medical Condition
Acute symptoms of sufficient severity (may include severe pain) such that absence of immediate medical attention could reasonably be expected to result in (A) serious jeopardy to patient health, (B) serious impairment to bodily functions, or (C) serious dysfunction of any bodily organ or part. EMC certified (by MD, DO, PA, or ARNP, at the first visit) → $10,000 benefit; not certified → $2,500 cap. The single most consequential clinical determination in the system.
OIR-B1-1571
Florida OIR “Standard Disclosure and Acknowledgment Form” — patient attests services were explained and actually rendered at the initial visit; dual patient + provider signatures; reimbursement prerequisite.
Patient Log
Chronological per-visit patient-signature record required for follow-up reimbursement under FL §627.736.
AOB
Assignment of Benefits — patient assigns insurance payment rights to the clinic (intake checklist item).
Excluded services
Massage therapy and acupuncture are not covered by Florida PIP; separately acknowledged and self-paid (Reception Form 6).
Fraud statute
FL §627.409 — false/incomplete/misleading insurance statements = third-degree felony; printed on the Sworn Affidavit.
Telehealth consent
FL §456.47 — Florida telehealth informed consent; documented with platform (doxy.me), patient location, persons present, synchronous A/V confirmation.
HIPAA
45 CFR §164 — role-based, audit-logged access; MRN built from SS4/ID4 instead of full SSN; duplicate-override reasons logged to the audit trail.
CMS-1500 / NUCC 02/12
The standard paper claim form; PI Master pre-audits ~25 box-level checks (hard stops vs soft warnings) before any package can be built.
Peer review defense
Language deliberately embedded in PT notes to survive insurer peer review — objective deficits, measurable progress, regression-if-discontinued reasoning.
PIP Only vs BI Litigation
The two case tracks. PIP Only = standard SOAP, functional ROM, CMS-1500, simple discharge. BI (bodily injury) Litigation = full defensive documentation: Waddell’s Signs, FCE trigger, causation documentation, MMI impairment rating, litigation package unlocked at MMI.
LOP
Letter of Protection — attorney’s promise to pay from settlement; specialist directory marks who accepts LOP / PIP / BI / Cash.

7Inconsistencies Flagged for Physician / Compliance Review

Found during the audit. None were altered (read-only audit); each is a decision the design owner should adjudicate before treating any single file as final truth.

#SeverityFinding
1HighEMC statutory citation drift. The EMC form cites “Florida Statute 627.409 / 627.4093”; the app-generated certification cites §627.732(4) and §627.736; the initial report attestation cites “FL 627.409.” §627.409 is the misrepresentation/fraud statute — the EMC definition lives in §627.732. Documents submitted to insurers should cite one correct section consistently.
2MedStaff scope conflict. Workflow specs repeatedly state staff enters “Section 1 only (demographics)” and cannot see medical data; the built MD Visit Module gives the staff phase three steps including accident mechanism and generation of legal documents (EMC among them). Decide which model governs.
3MedE/M code sets differ by artifact. Superbill spec: initial 99203-25/99204-25, follow-up 99212–99215-25. App dx step (initial): 99205 / 99206 / 99204. App case data and fee list use 99203/99204/99213/99214. 99206 does not exist in CPT — likely intended 99205 MDM variant; needs correction.
4MedImaging CPT drift. Cervical: 72050 (workflow specs, 3-view AP/Lat/Odontoid) vs 72052 (app rules engine, AP/Lat/Obliques). Knee: 73560 (fee list) vs 73562 (rules engine). Hip: 73510 vs 73502. View counts should match the billed code.
5MedShoulder sprain code variants. App uses S43.421A; the improved initial template uses S43.401A (unspecified part, right); knee similarly S83.401A vs S83.90XA. Pick canonical codes.
6LowHeadache code split. Generators use G44.309 (post-traumatic headache); mock case data and billing tables use G44.1 (“vascular headache NEC” — actually complicated headache syndromes). G44.309 matches the clinical intent.
7LowFee schedule divergence. BillingCodesScreen/superbill fees (99203 $175, 97012 $35…) differ from the billing-package charge map (99203 $150, 97010 $25, 97140 $60) and the superbill spec example ($250 for 99203). One master fee schedule should drive all three.
