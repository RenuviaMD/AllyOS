# RenuviaMD™ Men’s Hormone Optimization Clinical Protocol v0.2

**Date updated:** 2026-06-30  
**Scope:** Internal clinical protocol for men’s testosterone-centered wellness optimization.  
**Positioning:** Wellness practice, not narrow deficiency-only clinic.  
**Clinical safety rule:** Wellness goals may trigger evaluation; testosterone requires medical eligibility and safety review.  
**Dosing:** Not included. Use locked provider prescription protocol only.

---

## 1. Core philosophy

RenuviaMD men’s HRT is a **medical wellness optimization program**. The clinical entry point is not “treat a lab value.” The entry point is structured review of sexual vitality, energy/drive, body composition, strength/recovery, mood/cognition, sleep/recovery biology, and cardiometabolic longevity.

Testosterone is one possible physician-directed tool when the clinical picture, labs, safety screen, fertility goals, and monitoring readiness support its use.

## 2. Wellness phenotype map

| Phenotype | Typical presentation | Key differential |
|---|---|---|
| Sexual vitality | low libido, fewer morning erections, ED, low confidence | vascular ED, medication effects, depression, sleep apnea, relationship stress |
| Energy/drive | fatigue, low motivation, afternoon crash | sleep debt, OSA, thyroid, anemia, depression, insulin resistance |
| Body composition | abdominal fat, poor lean mass response | insulin resistance, diet, alcohol, sedentary pattern, GLP use |
| Recovery | poor training recovery, soreness, low strength response | undertraining/overtraining, low protein, sleep, vitamin D, anemia |
| Mood/cognition | irritability, low mood, brain fog | depression/anxiety, sleep, substances, medications |
| Sleep | snoring, witnessed apnea, non-restorative sleep | OSA must be addressed before testosterone pathway |
| Cardiometabolic | BP, waist, ApoB/LDL, A1c/insulin, fatty liver risk | lifestyle and metabolic optimization first-line |

## 4. Testosterone optimization eligibility outputs

| Output | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `FOUNDATIONAL_OPTIMIZATION_FIRST` | Wellness goals present but testosterone eligibility incomplete or not supported. |
| `LAB_MAPPING_REQUIRED` | Symptoms/goals present but key hormone/safety labs missing. |
| `FERTILITY_PRESERVING_REVIEW` | Fertility desired or unclear; do not auto-clear exogenous testosterone. |
| `TESTOSTERONE_OPTIMIZATION_CANDIDATE_REVIEW` | Symptoms/signs and repeat morning testosterone pattern align; safety gates still pass. |
| `HOLD_PENDING_SAFETY_REVIEW` | Hematocrit, prostate/PSA, BP/CV, OSA/LUTS, fertility, or safety gate active. |
| `DO_NOT_ROUTE_TO_TESTOSTERONE` | Unsupported goal-only or contraindication-level issue without review. |

## 5. Safety gates

Run all gates before testosterone pathway: wellness-only/no medical eligibility, fertility, prostate/PSA/BPH, hematocrit/polycythemia, CV/BP, untreated severe OSA, severe LUTS/BPH, and unsupported marketing-language gate.

## 6. Fertility pathway

If fertility is desired, do not automatically start exogenous testosterone. Document fertility timeline, consider semen analysis and reproductive/urology pathway when appropriate, and route to physician review of fertility-preserving strategies.

## 7. Monitoring

Monitor symptom goals, sexual function, energy, drive, strength/recovery, mood, cognition, adverse effects, BP, hematocrit, testosterone level per protocol, estradiol if symptoms/aromatization risk, PSA/prostate context when age/risk appropriate, metabolic markers, body composition, and continued benefit-risk ratio.

## 8. Language rules

Allowed: “This is a physician-guided men’s hormone optimization program. Testosterone may be considered when your goals, symptoms, labs, safety profile, fertility plans, and monitoring plan align.”

Blocked: testosterone for anti-aging; testosterone for energy only; testosterone melts fat; testosterone treats diabetes; safe for everyone; no monitoring needed; pellets/compounded therapy is automatically superior.

## 9. Documentation statement

“Patient presents for men’s hormone optimization and wellness evaluation with goals of [goals]. Testosterone therapy was not assumed. The evaluation reviewed symptoms, hormone-axis labs, fertility intent, hematocrit, prostate/PSA context, BP/CV status, OSA/LUTS risk, metabolic profile, and monitoring readiness. Plan selected after physician benefit-risk review and shared decision-making.”
