**Document type:** Patient-facing wellness plan template  
**Scope:** Testosterone-centered men’s hormone optimization, metabolic wellness, libido, energy, body composition, recovery, and longevity support

## 1. Your primary wellness goals

Your plan starts with your goals, not with a single lab number.

| Domain | Examples |
|---|---|
| Sexual vitality | Libido, erections, morning erections, sexual confidence |
| Energy and drive | Fatigue, motivation, afternoon crash, low stamina |
| Body composition | Abdominal fat, weight-loss resistance, lower lean mass |
| Strength and recovery | Reduced training response, soreness, slower recovery |
| Mood and cognition | Irritability, brain fog, low mood, low confidence |
| Sleep and recovery biology | Poor sleep, snoring, non-restorative sleep, possible sleep apnea |
| Cardiometabolic longevity | Blood pressure, glucose, insulin resistance, ApoB/LDL, waist circumference |

## 2. What your labs are designed to show

Labs are not used only to “prove low testosterone.” They map your hormone axis, metabolic health, safety profile, and optimization targets.

| Category | Key data |
|---|---|
| Androgen axis | Total testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG |
| Pituitary-gonadal signal | LH, FSH |
| Estrogen balance | Sensitive estradiol when clinically indicated |
| Adrenal vitality | DHEA-S when clinically useful |
| Safety | CBC/hematocrit, CMP, BP |
| Prostate context | PSA / prostate risk when age/risk appropriate |
| Metabolic health | HbA1c, fasting insulin, lipids, ApoB, waist circumference |
| Fatigue differential | TSH/free T4, B12, vitamin D, ferritin/iron when indicated |

## 3. What testosterone optimization can and cannot do

Testosterone may support selected men when symptoms, labs, safety profile, fertility goals, and physician assessment align.

It is **not** a shortcut for weight loss alone, anti-aging, gym performance alone, diabetes treatment, fatigue without evaluation, poor sleep caused by untreated sleep apnea, or lifestyle factors that need correction.

> Your energy, libido, recovery, and body-composition goals qualify you for a men’s hormone optimization evaluation. Testosterone therapy is considered only after your physician reviews symptoms, morning testosterone pattern, fertility goals, prostate/BP/hematocrit safety, sleep apnea risk, and expected benefit.

## 4. Safety review before testosterone is considered

Your physician reviews fertility goals, hematocrit/polycythemia risk, prostate cancer history, PSA, prostate symptoms, blood pressure, cardiovascular history, recent heart attack or stroke, thrombophilia/clotting risk, untreated severe sleep apnea, severe urinary symptoms/BPH, medication interactions, and substance use.

## 5. The RenuviaMD optimization plan

Every men’s program includes hormone-axis mapping, metabolic-risk review, sleep and recovery screen, nutrition/body-composition strategy, resistance-training plan, supplement review, physician decision on testosterone eligibility, and monitoring.

## 6. Foundational optimization plan

### Nutrition
Protein-forward Mediterranean-style diet. Emphasize lean protein, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, legumes, olive oil, vegetables, berries, oats, beans, and high-fiber carbohydrates. Reduce ultra-processed foods, added sugars, excess alcohol, and late-night eating.

### Exercise
Resistance training 3–4 days per week, Zone 2 cardiovascular work 150–300 minutes per week, optional interval work only when safe, and mobility/recovery work 2–3 days per week.

### Sleep
Screen for snoring, witnessed apnea, morning headaches, daytime sleepiness, and non-restorative sleep. Untreated severe sleep apnea must be evaluated before testosterone therapy is considered.

## 7. Supplement support

Possible physician-reviewed wellness supports include vitamin D, magnesium, omega-3, creatine monohydrate, protein/amino support, and zinc only when deficiency or dietary risk is present. Supplements do not replace medical evaluation or testosterone eligibility review.

## 8. Monitoring timeline

| Timing | What happens |
|---|---|
| Initial visit | Goals, symptoms, safety screen, fertility goals, vitals, labs ordered |
| Lab review | Hormone-axis and metabolic map reviewed; safety gates applied |
| 6–8 weeks | Early symptom and safety check if therapy started; foundational plan reviewed |
| 12 weeks | Body composition, energy/libido score, BP, hematocrit and hormone follow-up as appropriate |
| 6 months | Full benefit-risk review |
| Annual | Continued monitoring and shared decision-making |

## 9. When to contact the clinic promptly

Chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting, severe headache, neurologic symptoms, new leg swelling or calf pain, severe worsening urinary symptoms, new breast mass or concerning prostate/PSA issue, severe acne, mood instability, aggression, symptoms of very high blood pressure, or change in fertility plans.

## 10. Physician authorization

This plan is educational and informational. Prescription therapy requires physician review, appropriate safety screening, individualized benefit-risk assessment, and ongoing monitoring.
