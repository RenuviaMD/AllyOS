01

AOD-9604

Metabolism · Investigational · Grade D · Research-only

Indication

Adjunct fat-loss support within a structured metabolic/weight program. <!-- NOTE: human efficacy data weak; modest at best -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity to peptide/diluent
  • Active malignancy (precautionary)

Rx

  • Dose: 300 mcg SC daily; range 250–500 mcg/day
  • Route: SC (fasted/AM)
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 2.5 mg/mL = 2,500 mcg/mL → 300 mcg = 0.12 mL = 12 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 8–12 weeks
  • Stack pairs: tesamorelin, cjc-ipamorelin-blend, mots-c

Labs (baseline)

  • CMP, fasting glucose, lipids, weight/waist

Ongoing care

WhenWhat to checkWhat to adjust
Wk 2Tolerance, siteContinue
Mo 1Weight/waist trendHold or ↑ within range
Mo 3Body composition, lipidsContinue if benefit
Mo 6Weight maintenanceStop if no objective change

Safety & compliance gate

  • Investigational consent signed (`consent_investigational_peptide`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_aod-9604`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Baseline metabolic panel documented

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; effects are modest and adjunctive.
  • Does not replace diet and exercise — those drive results.
  • Doesn't affect blood sugar or IGF-1.
  • Report injection-site reactions or unexpected symptoms.

Evidence

Phase 2 obesity development **discontinued for lack of meaningful efficacy**; **zero registered trials**; substantive data preclinical.

References

  • Heffernan 2001, Endocrinology (preclinical)
  • Wilding 2004, Curr Opin Investig Drugs (review, PMID 15134286)

02

BPC-157

Repair · Research-only · Grade D · Research-only
FDA PCAC — 2026-07-23. Under Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee review for the 503A compounding list — non-binding, not FDA approval.

Indication

Adjunct for refractory soft-tissue (tendon/ligament/muscle) injury or GI mucosal complaints that have plateaued on standard care.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active or prior malignancy (pro-angiogenic — absolute)
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity to peptide/diluent
  • Undiagnosed mass / malignancy red flag

Rx

  • Dose: 250 mcg SC daily; range 250–500 mcg/day
  • Route: SC (local to injury preferred)
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 2.5 mg/mL = 2,500 mcg/mL → 250 mcg = 0.10 mL = 10 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 4–6 weeks, then reassess and stop
  • Stack pairs: tb-500, ghk-cu

Labs (baseline)

  • CBC, CMP
  • Age-appropriate cancer screening current ← gating

Ongoing care

WhenWhat to checkWhat to adjust
Wk 2Tolerance, injection site, response↑ to 500 mcg or stop if no signal
Mo 1Functional outcome (ROM/pain)Continue short course or discontinue
Mo 3Should be off; re-exam ± imagePivot to definitive care if unresolved
Mo 6Malignancy ROSStop + work up any red flag

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label/investigational consent signed (`consent_investigational_peptide`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_bpc-157`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Cancer screening current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; animal data only, no human efficacy proof.
  • Theoretical cancer-promotion risk — tell us any cancer history.
  • This supports rehab; it does not replace PT or needed surgery.
  • Report any new lump, weight loss, or unusual symptom immediately.

Evidence

No completed published human efficacy RCT. One Phase 1 safety/PK (NCT02637284, status unknown); one Phase 2 *recruiting* (NCT07437547, hamstring strain). FDA placed it in 503A Category 2, then removed it on Apr 15 2026 (not yet on the positive bulks list); PCAC review scheduled Jul 23–24 2026 — VERIFY current status.

References

  • Gwyer 2019, Cell Tissue Res
  • Seiwerth 2021, Front Pharmacol
  • Sikiric 2020, Curr Pharm Des

03

Cerebrolysin

Cognition · Research-only · Grade B/C · Physician-review

Indication

Cognitive support in post-stroke, TBI, or neurodegenerative decline. <!-- NOTE: EU/RU/AR-labeled; not FDA-approved -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Severe renal impairment
  • Status epilepticus / uncontrolled epilepsy

Rx

  • Dose: 5–10 mL daily ×10–20 day course
  • Route: IM (≤5 mL) or slow IV (larger volumes)
  • Reconstitution: Pre-mixed ampoule (215.2 mg/mL solution) — no reconstitution
  • Cycle: 10–20 day course, repeat 2–4×/year
  • Stack pairs: semax, selank, dsip

Labs (baseline)

  • CMP (renal), CBC
  • Seizure/neuro history review

Ongoing care

WhenWhat to checkWhat to adjust
Wk 2Tolerance, cognitionComplete course
Mo 1Cognitive/functional changeReassess
Mo 3Off-cycle statusPlan next course
Mo 6Durable benefitContinue vs stop

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label/investigational consent signed (`consent_investigational_peptide`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_cerebrolysin`)
  • Source pharmacy verified (counterfeit risk)
  • Renal function + seizure history documented

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved in the US; used as IV/IM courses.
  • Porcine-derived — tell us about allergies.
  • Report any seizure activity or dizziness.
  • Source authenticity matters — we verify supply.

Evidence

42 trials + meta-analyses, **mixed**: favorable Alzheimer's meta-analysis + positive post-stroke RCT (CARS), but a **stroke meta-analysis found no significant efficacy.** Approved in several non-US countries.

References

  • Muresanu 2016, Stroke (CARS)
  • Gauthier 2015, Dement Geriatr Cogn Disord (AD meta)
  • Zhang 2017, Biomed Res Int (negative stroke meta)

CJC-1295

Growth · Investigational · Grade C (DAC) / D (NO-DAC) · Research-only

Indication

GH-axis support for recovery and body composition, paired with a ghrelin agonist. <!-- NOTE: card assumes no-DAC (mod GRF 1-29), daily; DAC version is 1-2 mg weekly -->

  • Cycle: 12–16 weeks
  • Stack pairs: ipamorelin, sermorelin

  • Thyroid treated + cancer screen current ← gating

  • Not FDA-approved; compounded GH secretagogue.
  • Bedtime, empty stomach.

Cognition · Research-only · Grade D · Research-only
FDA PCAC — 2026-07-24. Under Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee review for the 503A compounding list — non-binding, not FDA approval.

Rx

  • Dose: 100–300 mcg SC at bedtime
  • Route: SC
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 2.5 mg/mL = 2,500 mcg/mL → 200 mcg = 0.08 mL = 8 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 4–6 weeks
  • Stack pairs: selank, cerebrolysin

Labs (baseline)

  • Generally none required
  • Sleep history / OSA screen

Ongoing care

WhenWhat to checkWhat to adjust

Copper tripeptide-1 (GHK-Cu)

Repair · Research-only · Grade C TOPICAL / D SYSTEMIC · Research-only

Indication

Skin and connective-tissue repair, hair support, and aesthetic regeneration. <!-- NOTE: strongest data is topical; systemic SC dosing is empiric -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active or prior malignancy (pro-angiogenic — absolute)
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity / copper sensitivity
  • Wilson disease / copper-overload state

Rx

  • Dose: 1–2 mg SC daily (or topical 1–2% serum)
  • Route: SC or topical
  • Reconstitution: 50 mg + 5 mL BAC water = 10 mg/mL = 10,000 mcg/mL → 2 mg = 0.20 mL = 20 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 4–8 weeks
  • Stack pairs: bpc-157, tb-500

Labs (baseline)

  • CBC, CMP
  • Serum copper/ceruloplasmin if chronic systemic use
  • Age-appropriate cancer screening current ← gating

Ongoing care

WhenWhat to checkWhat to adjust
Wk 2Skin/site toleranceContinue
Mo 1Response, copper if dosing highAdjust dose/route
Mo 3Outcome, serum copperTaper or stop
Mo 6Malignancy ROS, copper statusStop on any red flag/overload

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label/investigational consent signed (`consent_investigational_peptide`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_ghk-cu`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Cancer screening current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved as an injectable; topical use is better studied.
  • Don't exceed dosing — copper can accumulate.
  • Tell us any cancer history.
  • Report rash, metallic taste, or unusual symptoms.

Evidence

Mostly topical/cosmetic + in-vitro. A small topical RCT post-laser showed improved satisfaction but **no objective benefit**. Ph2 topical RCT recruiting (NCT07437586). No completed RCT for injected/systemic use.

Bremelanotide

Vitality · FDA-approved · Grade A (LABEL INDICATION) · Grade A pathway

Indication

Acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women (on-label); off-label for ED or low desire.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Uncontrolled hypertension (absolute)
  • Significant cardiovascular disease
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Priapism-predisposing condition (ED use)

Rx

  • Dose: 1.75 mg SC PRN, ≥45 min before activity; max 1/24h, ≤8/month
  • Route: SC PRN
  • Reconstitution: Vyleesi autoinjector pre-mixed (1.75 mg/0.3 mL) — no math. Compounded: 10 mg + 5 mL = 2 mg/mL → 1.75 mg = 0.875 mL. <!-- NOTE: verify compounded concentration -->
  • Cycle: PRN ongoing; reassess at 8 wk
  • Stack pairs: kisspeptin-10

Labs (baseline)

  • BP documented controlled ← gating
  • Pregnancy test; reversible-cause screen (TSH, meds, mood)

Ongoing care

WhenWhat to checkWhat to adjust
Wk 2Nausea, BP, efficacyPre-treat nausea; confirm 45-min lead
Mo 1Efficacy, dose countKeep ≤8/month
Mo 3Benefit, BP, skinDiscontinue if no benefit by 8 wk
Mo 6Efficacy, BP, skinContinue if effective

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label consent signed (`consent_sexual_health_peptide`) — N/A if on-label
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_pt-141`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • BP controlled documented ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Nausea is common early — take ≥45 min before activity, with food.
  • Don't use if blood pressure is uncontrolled; report chest pain.
  • Repeated use can darken skin/gums (may not fully reverse).
