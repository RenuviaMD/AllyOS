# RenuviaMD™ Men’s Testosterone Optimization
## Target, Dosing & Administration Evidence Addendum v0.1

**Date updated:** 2026-06-30  
**Scope:** Wellness-focused men’s hormone optimization  
**Use:** Provider-facing protocol addendum  
**Positioning:** Testosterone-centered wellness medicine, not bodybuilding, anti-aging, or deficiency-only care.  
**Clinical rule:** Patient-specific dosing remains physician-directed.

---

## 1. Wellness target philosophy

For a wellness-focused men’s hormone optimization program, the goal is physiologic restoration, not supraphysiologic enhancement.

### Preferred RenuviaMD target

| Marker | Practical target |
|---|---|
| Total testosterone | **450–600 ng/dL preferred** |
| Acceptable monitored range | **~450–750 ng/dL** if symptoms improve and safety markers remain clean |
| Avoid | Sustained supraphysiologic levels, especially >900–1000 ng/dL |
| Free testosterone | Mid-normal to upper-mid lab reference range when SHBG is abnormal |
| Estradiol | Do not automatically suppress; monitor if gynecomastia, breast symptoms, edema, libido issues, obesity/aromatization risk |

AUA uses the middle tertile of the normal range, approximately **450–600 ng/dL**, as a therapeutic target. The Endocrine Society recommends aiming for the **mid-normal range**.

---

## 2. Wellness positioning

Do **not** frame the program as:

> “We treat a low testosterone lab.”

Use:

> “We optimize male vitality, libido, body composition, energy, recovery, sleep, and cardiometabolic health using a physician-guided hormone and wellness protocol. Testosterone is considered only when symptoms, labs, safety profile, fertility goals, and monitoring readiness align.”

Wellness goals can trigger evaluation, but testosterone should not be positioned as a shortcut for energy, cognition, gym performance, weight loss, anti-aging, or diabetes treatment.

---

## 3. Eligibility anchor

The wellness practice model starts with goals, but prescription testosterone still needs a medical eligibility gate.

Core logic:

1. Wellness goals and symptoms documented.
2. Morning testosterone pattern reviewed.
3. Repeat confirmation obtained when low or borderline.
4. Free testosterone and SHBG considered when relevant.
5. Fertility goals reviewed.
6. Safety gates cleared or routed for review.
7. Provider documents benefit-risk and shared decision-making.

## 4. Administration options

### A. Topical gel / cream

Best wellness use:

- conservative first-line option
- older patients
- higher hematocrit concern
- patients where reversibility matters
- patients avoiding injections

Evidence-based label examples:

| Product style | Label framework |
|---|---|
| Testosterone gel 1.62% / AndroGel 1.62% style | Start **40.5 mg once daily** in the morning; adjust **20.25–81 mg/day** based on pre-dose morning testosterone |
| Testosterone gel 1% | Start **50 mg daily**; adjust to **75 mg** or **100 mg** based on testosterone level |

Application principles:

- clean, dry skin
- shoulders / upper arms depending product
- wash hands after application
- cover site after drying
- prevent transfer to partners, children, or pets

Pros: smoother exposure, easy to stop, conservative start.  
Cons: transfer risk, variable absorption, daily adherence.

---

### B. Weekly subcutaneous testosterone enanthate auto-injector

| Product style | Label framework |
|---|---|
| Testosterone enanthate SC auto-injector / XYOSTED style | Start **75 mg subcutaneously once weekly** |
| Monitoring target | Trough **350–650 ng/dL**, measured 7 days after dose |
| Adjustment | Increase/decrease by 25 mg based on trough |

Best wellness use:

- patients preferring weekly dosing
- more predictable delivery than topical absorption
- clean protocolization

Cautions: BP, hematocrit, prostate/PSA context, fertility, and OSA/LUTS monitoring still apply.

---

### C. Testosterone cypionate / enanthate injections

| Product style | Label framework |
|---|---|
| Testosterone cypionate IM | **50–400 mg every 2–4 weeks** |
| Testosterone enanthate IM | **50–400 mg every 2–4 weeks** |

Wellness interpretation:

Large infrequent injections can cause peaks, troughs, mood changes, estradiol symptoms, and hematocrit pressure.

For optimization, prefer:

> smaller, more frequent dosing rather than large every-2-to-4-week dosing.

AUA gives the practical example that **100 mg weekly** is preferred to **200 mg every 2 weeks** or **300–400 mg monthly**, because it creates more stable levels.

---

### D. Oral testosterone undecanoate

| Product style | Label framework |
|---|---|
| Oral testosterone undecanoate | Start **200 mg twice daily with food** |
| Monitoring | Check testosterone **7 days** after start or adjustment, **3–5 hours after morning dose** |
| Range | Minimum 100 mg once daily; maximum 400 mg twice daily depending product label |

Best wellness use:

- avoids injection
- avoids topical transfer
- useful when food-dependent dosing is acceptable

Cautions: BP monitoring, absorption variability, cost/access.

---

### E. Testosterone pellets

| Product style | Label framework |
|---|---|
| Testosterone pellets / Testopel style | **150–450 mg subcutaneously every 3–6 months** |

RenuviaMD posture:

> Pellets are not first-line for initial optimization. They are better considered only after stable response, stable safety markers, and clear patient preference for long-duration therapy.

Pros: convenience.  
Cons: low reversibility, procedure risk, extrusion/infection risk, difficult dose adjustment.

---

## 5. Preferred route hierarchy

### First-line wellness options

1. **Topical gel / cream** — conservative, reversible, smooth exposure.
2. **Weekly low-peak injectable pathway** — useful when topical is not ideal.
3. **Subcutaneous auto-injector pathway** — defined trough target and clean protocolization.

### Selected-use options

4. **Oral testosterone undecanoate** — convenient but BP and absorption considerations matter.
5. **Pellets** — premium convenience but least flexible; not preferred for first initiation.

## 6. Monitoring targets and safety stops

| Marker | Protocol rule |
|---|---|
| Total testosterone | Target **450–600 ng/dL preferred** |
| Free testosterone | Lab-specific mid to upper-mid normal when clinically aligned |
| Hematocrit | Baseline required; hold/stop/review if **>54%** |
| BP | Monitor every visit; testosterone class labeling includes BP warning |
| PSA/prostate | Baseline when age/risk appropriate; monitor after start |
| Estradiol | Check if gynecomastia, breast symptoms, edema, libido issue, or aromatization risk |
| Fertility | Do not start exogenous testosterone if trying to conceive without fertility-preserving review |
| OSA | Untreated severe OSA is a hold/review gate |
| LUTS/BPH | Severe LUTS requires review/monitoring |

## 7. 2025–2026 safety update

Current evidence is more balanced than older fear-based cardiovascular/prostate language, but testosterone is not risk-free.

### FDA 2025 update

FDA updated class-wide testosterone labeling after TRAVERSE and ambulatory blood pressure studies. The update added/revised blood-pressure warning language and incorporated cardiovascular-safety findings.

Clinical implication:

> CV discussion can be more balanced, but BP and cardiovascular monitoring remain mandatory.

### HHS/FDA 2026 requested update

HHS/FDA requested label updates regarding age-related hypogonadism, prostate cancer risk, and BPH warnings. Screening and monitoring remain recommended, especially in severe symptomatic BPH contexts.

Clinical implication:

> Do not overstate prostate risk, but do not remove prostate/PSA/BPH monitoring.

## 8. Language guardrails

Allowed:

> “Testosterone optimization may be considered when symptoms, labs, safety profile, fertility goals, and physician assessment align. The goal is physiologic restoration into a monitored mid-normal range, not supraphysiologic enhancement.”

Blocked:

- TRT is anti-aging.
- TRT is for all men over 40.
- TRT melts fat.
- TRT treats diabetes.
- TRT is safe for everyone.
- TRT fixes fatigue without labs.
- No monitoring needed.
- Pellets are best.
- Compounded testosterone is safer.
- Target the highest possible testosterone.

---

## 9. Final RenuviaMD protocol position

> **Target total testosterone: 450–600 ng/dL preferred, with symptom improvement and clean safety markers.**

> **Use the lowest effective dose and route that improves the patient’s priority wellness goals while keeping hematocrit, BP, prostate context, fertility goals, estradiol symptoms, sleep apnea, and LUTS under control.**

> **Do not chase supraphysiologic levels.**
