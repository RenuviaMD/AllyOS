## 2. Standing order name
**Niagen® / Nicotinamide Riboside Chloride (NR) IV Infusion**

## 3. Clinical intent
Provider-reviewed NAD+ precursor support for selected adults in a wellness / fatigue-recovery /
cellular-energy / mitochondrial-support context. Must **not** be presented as treatment for disease,
anti-aging therapy, addiction therapy, neurodegenerative/metabolic disease, cancer, chronic fatigue
syndrome, or as proven support for peptide therapy.

## 4. Eligible patient profile (all must be true)
1. Adult ≥ 18. 2. Clinically stable at visit. 3. No acute red-flag symptoms. 4. Vitals reviewed and
acceptable per clinic policy. 5. Medication/allergy history reviewed. 6. Provider-reviewed rationale
(fatigue-recovery, wellness cellular-energy, mitochondrial/longevity support, or informed patient
preference). 7. Patient understands outcome evidence is preliminary and no peptide-combination benefit
is proven.

## 5. Hold / defer / physician-review criteria
**Hard hold pending physician decision:** pregnancy or suspected pregnancy; lactation (unless physician
documents rationale); age < 18; known hypersensitivity to NR/Niagen®/excipients or prior NAD/NR
infusion reaction; active chest pain, dyspnea at rest, syncope, unstable arrhythmia, severe tachycardia,
severe hypertension/hypotension, hypoxia, fever/sepsis symptoms, acute neurologic symptoms, altered
mental status; active infusion reaction (rash, urticaria, wheezing, throat tightness, angioedema, chest
pressure, severe cramping, severe nausea/vomiting, presyncope, or significant BP/HR change).

**Evidence-gap physician review required:** active/recent malignancy or active oncology treatment;
significant hepatic disease or unexplained elevated LFTs; advanced CKD / dialysis / unstable renal
function or abnormal BUN/creatinine; immunocompromise or complex inflammatory disease; uncontrolled
diabetes or unstable metabolic disease; polypharmacy with incomplete medication list; any request for
repeated or higher-dose treatment beyond this protocol.

## 6. Baseline assessment
**Before each infusion:** documented indication/rationale; allergy history; current medication and
supplement list; focused symptom screen; pregnancy status when relevant; vitals (BP, HR, RR, SpO2,
temperature); IV access assessment.
**Labs:** not mandatory for a single low-risk infusion. For multi-day/repeated course, high-risk, or
abnormal history, review recent labs per policy. Evidence-aligned safety set (because these were the
domains measured in the 2026 study): CMP incl. ALT/AST and creatinine/BUN (eGFR if available);
fasting glucose and/or A1c if metabolic/GLP-1 context; lipid panel if tracking metabolic outcomes; TSH
and hsCRP only if a thyroid/inflammatory tracking protocol requires. These are safety/tracking domains —
**not** proof of benefit.

## 7. Dose / infusion order
**Evidence-supported default:** Niagen® / NR **500 mg IV in 500 mL 0.9% normal saline**, infused over
**30–45 minutes as tolerated.**
**Tolerability adjustment:** slow or pause for tongue/jaw/arm tingling, mild cramping, nausea, anxiety,
flushing-like symptoms, dizziness, or BP/HR change; resume only if resolved and clinically appropriate.
**Stop and escalate** for chest pressure, dyspnea, throat tightness, urticaria, wheezing, angioedema,
severe cramping, syncope/presyncope, severe hypertension/hypotension, persistent tachycardia, or any
concerning reaction.
**Frequency — evidence-supported options only:** (1) single 500 mg IV; or (2) 500 mg IV daily for up to
**4 consecutive days**, provider-approved with documented rationale + monitoring.
**Not evidence-supported in this draft:** routine indefinite weekly/monthly maintenance, or 750 mg /
1,000 mg / higher per-session NR IV. These may exist commercially but are not audited standing-order
doses here.

## 8. Administration notes
Aseptic IV technique and standard monitoring. Do **not** mix NR/Niagen with other IV nutrients/meds in the
same bag unless pharmacy/manufacturer compatibility documentation exists and the Medical Director has
approved it in writing. Do **not** label NR/Niagen as the same product as NAD+ IV (NR is a precursor). Do
**not** add medication add-ons unless covered by a separate standing order.

## 9. Monitoring during infusion
Document start/stop time; baseline and post-infusion BP/HR; symptoms during infusion; any
interruption/rate-reduction/discontinuation; any adverse reaction + intervention; disposition. Expected
minor transient symptoms include tongue/jaw/arm tingling and mild cramping. Absence of serious events in
small studies does **not** prove broad safety.

## 10. Post-infusion instructions
Instruct the patient to report: chest pain/pressure; shortness of breath; rash/hives/facial-lip-tongue
swelling/throat tightness; persistent vomiting or severe abdominal cramping; syncope/presyncope; new
severe neurologic symptoms; any delayed reaction.

## 11. Documentation statement (suggested chart phrase)
"Patient received provider-reviewed Niagen® / nicotinamide riboside chloride IV infusion for NAD+
precursor support. Evidence reviewed: limited human NR IV data support 500 mg IV dosing with preliminary
tolerability data; no disease-treatment or peptide-synergy claims were made. Risks, benefits, alternatives,
and evidence limitations reviewed. Vitals and symptom monitoring completed."

## 12. Claims the AI / clinic must NOT make
"Proven anti-aging" · "Reverses aging" · "Treats chronic fatigue" · "Treats addiction" · "Treats
neurodegenerative disease" · "Detoxifies the body" · "Makes peptides work better" · "Equivalent to NAD+
IV" · "Superior clinical outcome vs NAD+ IV" · "Safe in all patients."

## 13. Source audit summary
*(⚠ UNDER AllyOS citation audit — status PENDING at the time of this draft. Each citation below will be
confirmed, reworded, or pulled based on the audit before this standing order is locked into the module.)*
1. **Reyna et al., Frontiers in Aging, 2026** — retrospective commercial-setting tolerability study; 500 mg
   NR IV (or NAD+ IV) × 4 consecutive days; NR mean infusion 37 ± 13 min; minor transient symptoms; no
   significant ALT/AST, hsCRP, BUN/creatinine, or TSH change at 30 days. Limitations: small, retrospective,
   commercial context, no placebo/matched control.
2. **Hawkins et al., medRxiv, 2024** — randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled **preprint** (not peer
   reviewed); single 500 mg NR IV vs 500 mg NAD+ IV, oral NR, and saline; preliminary.
3. **Restore (Niagen/NR IV) public page** — commercial source; market availability + 30–45 min service
   duration + up-to-4,000 mg/28-day commercial-use statement. **Not** used as efficacy evidence.
4. **Niagen Plus public page** — manufacturer/commercial source; product identity (NR chloride) +
   clinician-administered IV/IM availability. **Not** independent efficacy evidence.
5. **Conze et al., 2019, Scientific Reports** — **oral** NR safety/metabolism (100/300/1000 mg/day × 8 wk);
   background only; does **not** validate IV route/dose/time.
