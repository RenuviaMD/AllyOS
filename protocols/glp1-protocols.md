# GLP-1 / Incretin Agent Clinical Management Protocols

**For use by LICENSED prescribers as decision-support only.** This is not a substitute for the full FDA prescribing information or independent clinical judgment. Confirm doses against the current label before prescribing. Dosing below reflects the **anti-obesity (chronic weight management)** brands unless otherwise stated; the same molecules carry separate diabetes brands/labels with different titration.

**Class boxed warning (applies to semaglutide, tirzepatide, liraglutide; presumed for retatrutide):** Thyroid C-cell tumors / medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) in rodents. **Contraindicated** in patients with a personal or family history of MTC or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2). Counsel on symptoms of thyroid tumor (neck mass, dysphagia, dyspnea, persistent hoarseness).

**Shared class contraindications/cautions:** prior serious hypersensitivity to the agent; pregnancy (discontinue — see each agent; weight loss offers no benefit and may cause fetal harm); history of pancreatitis (use caution); avoid concomitant use with other GLP-1 based products.

## 1. SEMAGLUTIDE (weight management brand: Wegovy 2.4 mg SC weekly)

### 1. Candidacy / indication
- **On-label (chronic weight management), adults:** BMI **≥30** (obesity), OR BMI **≥27** (overweight) **plus ≥1 weight-related comorbidity** (e.g., hypertension, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia). Also approved for adolescents ≥12 yr at the ≥95th BMI percentile; to reduce MACE in adults with established CV disease and obesity/overweight; **noncirrhotic MASH with moderate–advanced (F2–F3) fibrosis (Wegovy, Aug 2025)**; and as **oral semaglutide 25 mg once daily for chronic weight management (Dec 2025)**.
- Adjunct to reduced-calorie diet + increased physical activity.
- **Off-label:** use below these BMI thresholds, or use of the diabetes brand (Ozempic) for weight loss, is off-label. Document rationale.

### 2. Starting dose
- **0.25 mg SC once weekly** for 4 weeks (sub-therapeutic; for tolerability/initiation only).

### 3. Titration schedule (Wegovy)
| Step | Dose (SC weekly) | Duration before next step |
|------|------------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | 0.25 mg | 4 weeks |
| 2 | 0.5 mg | 4 weeks |
| 3 | 1.0 mg | 4 weeks |
| 4 | 1.7 mg | 4 weeks |
| 5 (maintenance) | **2.4 mg** | ongoing (max) |
- **Maximum dose: 2.4 mg weekly.** Escalate at ~4-week intervals.
- **HOLD / SLOW rule:** If a patient does not tolerate a dose, **delay escalation by 4 weeks at the current (or prior) dose** — do not skip steps and do not advance on schedule through active GI symptoms. If the 2.4 mg maintenance dose is not tolerated, the label permits temporarily decreasing to 1.7 mg for up to 4 weeks then re-attempting 2.4 mg. If 2.4 mg still not tolerated after re-attempt, **discontinue.** If ≥2 consecutive weekly doses are missed, consider reinitiating at a lower step and re-titrating per the patient's tolerance.

### 4. Adverse events to watch
- **COMMON (STEP 1 trial / label; approximate):** nausea **~44%**, diarrhea **~30%**, vomiting **~24%**, constipation **~24%**; also abdominal pain, dyspepsia, eructation, headache, fatigue. GI effects are usually mild–moderate, dose-escalation–related, and transient. Treatment discontinuation for GI events ~4.5% (vs 0.8% placebo) in STEP 1.
- **SERIOUS — counsel on warning signs:**
  - **Acute pancreatitis** — severe, persistent abdominal pain ± radiating to back, ± vomiting.
  - **Gallbladder disease/cholelithiasis** — RUQ pain, fever, jaundice, clay-colored stools.
  - **Acute kidney injury** from volume depletion (via vomiting/diarrhea) — decreased urine, lethargy.
  - **Diabetic retinopathy** complications (counsel patients with pre-existing retinopathy/T2DM).
  - **Hypoglycemia** when combined with insulin or a sulfonylurea.
  - **MTC / thyroid C-cell** (boxed warning) — neck mass, hoarseness, dysphagia.
  - Hypersensitivity/anaphylaxis; heart-rate increase; suicidal ideation/behavior (monitor mood).

### 5. Side-effect MANAGEMENT
- **Nausea/vomiting:** smaller, more frequent low-fat meals; eat slowly, stop when full; avoid greasy/spicy/very sweet foods; stay hydrated. **Hold escalation** (stay at current dose +4 wk) rather than pushing through. Short-course antiemetic (e.g., ondansetron) for breakthrough nausea if needed. Persistent severe vomiting → step down a dose.
- **Constipation:** increase dietary fiber and fluids, physical activity; add osmotic laxative (e.g., polyethylene glycol) or stool softener as needed.
- **Hypoglycemia:** at initiation in patients on insulin/sulfonylurea, **reduce the insulin/SU dose** and monitor glucose; counsel on hypoglycemia recognition/treatment.
- **Gallbladder symptoms:** evaluate (RUQ ultrasound, LFTs) if biliary symptoms develop.
- **Suspected pancreatitis:** **STOP the drug promptly**, evaluate (lipase/amylase, clinical assessment). **If pancreatitis is confirmed, do NOT restart.**
- **Dehydration / AKI:** counsel on hydration; with significant GI losses, **hold the dose**, rehydrate, and check renal function (creatinine/eGFR); reassess concomitant diuretics/ACEi/ARB/NSAIDs.

### 6. Monitoring / follow-up schedule (CONTINUOUS therapy — no cycling/rest periods; follow-up only)
- **Baseline:** weight/BMI, BP, heart rate; renal function; glucose/HbA1c if diabetic/prediabetic; review meds (insulin/SU, diuretics); pregnancy status; screen MTC/MEN2 and pancreatitis history; mood/mental health baseline.
- **~4 weeks (and at each up-titration):** tolerability/GI symptoms, hydration, weight, HR/BP; reassess insulin/SU dose; mood.
- **3 months:** weight response (assess whether clinically meaningful loss is occurring), tolerability, BP/HR, glucose if diabetic.
- **6 months and ongoing (every 3–6 months):** weight trend, metabolic labs as indicated, ongoing GI tolerance, mental-health check.
- Discontinue if pregnancy occurs. Re-evaluate continuation if inadequate response after reaching maintenance dose.

### 7. Discontinuation & what to expect
- GLP-1 therapy is **chronic**; obesity is a chronic relapsing disease. On stopping, **substantial weight regain is expected** (STEP-extension/withdrawal data show roughly two-thirds of lost weight regained over ~1 year) and metabolic improvements attenuate.
- **No pharmacologic taper is mandated by the label** (the up-titration scheme is for initiation tolerability). If stopping, reinforce intensified lifestyle support; if restarting after a gap of ≥2 missed doses, re-initiate at a lower dose and re-titrate.

**Sources — Semaglutide:** FDA label, Wegovy (semaglutide) — DailyMed / FDA accessdata (**VERIFY current revision**). Pivotal trial: Wilding JPH, et al. *Once-Weekly Semaglutide in Adults with Overweight or Obesity* (STEP 1), N Engl J Med 2021;384:989-1002 — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2032183) (PubMed PMID 33567185).

## 2. TIRZEPATIDE (weight management brand: Zepbound SC weekly; GIP/GLP-1 dual agonist)

### 1. Candidacy / indication
- **On-label (chronic weight management), adults:** BMI **≥30**, OR BMI **≥27 plus ≥1 weight-related comorbidity** (e.g., hypertension, dyslipidemia, T2DM, OSA, CVD). Also approved for moderate-to-severe **obstructive sleep apnea** in adults with obesity.
- Adjunct to reduced-calorie diet + increased physical activity.
- **Off-label:** below thresholds, or using the diabetes brand (Mounjaro) for weight loss, is off-label.

### 2. Starting dose
- **2.5 mg SC once weekly** for 4 weeks (initiation dose; not for glycemic/weight efficacy).

### 3. Titration schedule (Zepbound)
| Step | Dose (SC weekly) | Duration before next step |
|------|------------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | 2.5 mg | 4 weeks |
| 2 | 5 mg | 4 weeks (this is the first maintenance option) |
| 3 | 7.5 mg | ≥4 weeks |
| 4 | 10 mg | ≥4 weeks |
| 5 | 12.5 mg | ≥4 weeks |
| 6 | **15 mg** | ongoing (max) |
- Increase by **2.5 mg increments after ≥4 weeks** on the current dose. **Maximum dose: 15 mg weekly.** **Maintenance doses are 5, 10, or 15 mg.**
- **HOLD / SLOW rule:** Do not escalate until the patient has been on the current dose ≥4 weeks AND tolerates it. If GI intolerance occurs, **stay at the current dose** (or step down) and delay escalation; do not skip increments. If ≥2 consecutive doses are missed, consider re-evaluation/re-titration per the label.

### 4. Adverse events to watch
- **COMMON (SURMOUNT-1 / label; approximate, pooled across doses):** nausea **~25–33%** (10 mg arm ~33%), diarrhea **~19–23%**, constipation **~11–17%**, vomiting **~8–13%** (all dose-related, mostly mild–moderate, mainly during escalation); also dyspepsia, abdominal pain, injection-site reactions, fatigue. Discontinuation for AEs ~4–7% across doses in SURMOUNT-1.
- **SERIOUS — counsel on warning signs:** acute **pancreatitis** (severe persistent abdominal pain ± back, vomiting); **gallbladder disease**/cholelithiasis; **acute kidney injury** from volume depletion; **hypoglycemia** with insulin/SU; **diabetic retinopathy** worsening in T2DM; hypersensitivity reactions; **MTC/thyroid C-cell** (boxed warning); suicidal ideation/behavior (monitor mood). **May reduce efficacy of oral hormonal contraceptives** around dose initiation/escalation — advise a non-oral backup or barrier method for 4 weeks after initiation and after each dose increase.

### 5. Side-effect MANAGEMENT
- **Nausea/vomiting:** dietary measures (small, low-fat meals; eat slowly; avoid trigger foods); hydrate; **hold escalation/stay at current dose** through symptoms; antiemetic (e.g., ondansetron) for breakthrough; step down if severe/persistent.
- **Constipation:** fiber, fluids, activity; osmotic laxative (polyethylene glycol)/stool softener.
- **Hypoglycemia:** reduce **insulin/sulfonylurea** at initiation; monitor glucose; educate on recognition/treatment.
- **Gallbladder symptoms:** evaluate with RUQ ultrasound and LFTs if biliary symptoms occur.
- **Suspected pancreatitis:** **STOP**, evaluate (lipase, clinical assessment); **if confirmed, do not restart.**
- **Dehydration / AKI:** with significant GI fluid losses, **hold dose**, rehydrate, check renal function; reassess diuretics/RAAS blockers/NSAIDs.

### 6. Monitoring / follow-up schedule (CONTINUOUS — no cycling/rest; follow-up only)
- **Baseline:** weight/BMI, BP, HR; renal function; glucose/HbA1c if dysglycemic; med review (insulin/SU, oral contraceptives, diuretics); pregnancy status; MTC/MEN2 and pancreatitis screen; mood baseline.
- **~4 weeks (and at each up-titration):** GI tolerability, hydration, weight, HR/BP; adjust insulin/SU; reinforce contraception counseling after each dose increase; mood.
- **3 months:** weight response, tolerability, BP/HR, glucose if diabetic.
- **6 months and ongoing (every 3–6 months):** weight trend, metabolic labs as indicated, GI tolerance, mental health.
- Discontinue if pregnancy occurs; reassess continuation if inadequate response at maintenance dose.

### 7. Discontinuation & what to expect
- Chronic therapy. **SURMOUNT-4** (withdrawal) showed continued tirzepatide maintained/extended weight loss whereas **switching to placebo led to substantial regain** — expect meaningful weight regain after stopping.
- **No mandated pharmacologic taper** (initiation up-titration is for tolerability). Intensify lifestyle support on discontinuation; re-titrate if restarting after a significant gap.

**Sources — Tirzepatide:** FDA label, Zepbound (tirzepatide) — DailyMed / FDA accessdata (**VERIFY current revision**). Pivotal trial: Jastreboff AM, et al. *Tirzepatide Once Weekly for the Treatment of Obesity* (SURMOUNT-1), N Engl J Med 2022;387:205-216 — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2206038) (PubMed PMID 35658024).

## 3. LIRAGLUTIDE (weight management brand: Saxenda 3.0 mg SC DAILY)

### 1. Candidacy / indication
- **On-label (chronic weight management), adults:** BMI **≥30**, OR BMI **≥27 plus ≥1 weight-related comorbidity** (e.g., hypertension, T2DM, dyslipidemia). Also approved for adolescents 12–17 yr with body weight >60 kg and obesity (BMI ≥95th percentile).
- Adjunct to reduced-calorie diet + increased physical activity.
- **Note:** liraglutide is **once DAILY** (not weekly), unlike semaglutide/tirzepatide. The diabetes brand (Victoza, max 1.8 mg) used for weight loss is off-label.

### 2. Starting dose
- **0.6 mg SC once daily** for 1 week.

### 3. Titration schedule (Saxenda) — weekly steps
| Step | Dose (SC daily) | Duration before next step |
|------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | 0.6 mg | 1 week |
| 2 | 1.2 mg | 1 week |
| 3 | 1.8 mg | 1 week |
| 4 | 2.4 mg | 1 week |
| 5 (maintenance) | **3.0 mg** | ongoing (max) |
- Escalate by **0.6 mg at weekly intervals** to the **maximum/maintenance dose of 3.0 mg daily.**
- **HOLD / SLOW rule:** If a dose-increase is not tolerated, **delay escalation by ~1 additional week** at the current dose. If the patient **cannot tolerate 3.0 mg, discontinue** (the label advises against staying long-term at lower doses for efficacy, since efficacy is at 3.0 mg). If ≥3 consecutive days are missed, re-initiate at 0.6 mg and re-titrate to limit GI effects.

### 4. Adverse events to watch
- **COMMON (SCALE Obesity and Prediabetes / label; approximate):** nausea **~39%**, diarrhea **~21%**, constipation **~19%**, vomiting **~16%**; also hypoglycemia (esp. with T2DM meds), dyspepsia, abdominal pain, headache, fatigue, dizziness, injection-site reactions. Mild–moderate and often early/transient.
- **SERIOUS — counsel on warning signs:** acute **pancreatitis** (severe persistent abdominal pain ± back/vomiting); **gallbladder disease**/cholelithiasis (higher background rate with rapid weight loss); **acute kidney injury** from volume depletion; **hypoglycemia** with insulin/SU; **heart-rate increase**; **diabetic retinopathy** in T2DM; hypersensitivity; **MTC/thyroid C-cell** (boxed warning); suicidal ideation/behavior (monitor mood).

### 5. Side-effect MANAGEMENT
- **Nausea/vomiting:** small, low-fat, frequent meals; eat slowly; avoid trigger foods; hydrate; **delay up-titration/stay at current dose** through symptoms; antiemetic for breakthrough; step down if severe.
- **Constipation:** fiber, fluids, activity; osmotic laxative/stool softener.
- **Hypoglycemia:** reduce **insulin/sulfonylurea** at initiation; monitor glucose; educate.
- **Gallbladder symptoms:** evaluate (RUQ ultrasound, LFTs) if biliary symptoms.
- **Suspected pancreatitis:** **STOP**, evaluate; **if confirmed, do not restart.**
- **Dehydration / AKI:** with GI fluid losses, **hold**, rehydrate, check renal function; reassess diuretics/RAAS/NSAIDs.

### 6. Monitoring / follow-up schedule (CONTINUOUS — no cycling/rest; follow-up only; standard GLP-1 cadence)
- **Baseline:** weight/BMI, BP, HR; renal function; glucose/HbA1c if dysglycemic; med review (insulin/SU, diuretics); pregnancy status; MTC/MEN2 and pancreatitis screen; mood baseline.
- **~4 weeks (after reaching/maintaining 3.0 mg):** GI tolerability, hydration, weight, HR/BP; adjust insulin/SU; mood. (Note up-titration itself is weekly.)
- **3 months:** the label specifies assessing weight loss at **~16 weeks (~4 months)**: if a patient has **not lost ≥4% of baseline body weight**, the drug is **unlikely to be effective — consider discontinuation.** Capture this milestone in the 3–4 month visit.
- **6 months and ongoing (every 3–6 months):** weight trend, metabolic labs as indicated, GI tolerance, mental health.
- Discontinue if pregnancy occurs.

### 7. Discontinuation & what to expect
- Chronic therapy; **weight regain is expected** after stopping (SCALE Maintenance and withdrawal data show partial-to-substantial regain).
- **No mandated taper** for efficacy reasons; the up-titration is for GI tolerability only. If restarting after ≥3 missed days, re-initiate at 0.6 mg and re-titrate. Intensify lifestyle support on discontinuation.

**Sources — Liraglutide:** FDA label, Saxenda (liraglutide) — DailyMed / FDA accessdata (**VERIFY current revision**; note 16-week/4% response rule and weekly up-titration). Pivotal trial: Pi-Sunyer X, et al. *A Randomized, Controlled Trial of 3.0 mg of Liraglutide in Weight Management* (SCALE Obesity and Prediabetes), N Engl J Med 2015;373:11-22 — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa1411892) (PubMed PMID 26132939).

## 4. RETATRUTIDE (LY3437943) — **INVESTIGATIONAL / PHASE 3 — NO FDA LABEL — VERIFY**

> **⚠ ENTIRE SECTION IS INVESTIGATIONAL.** As of the knowledge cutoff, retatrutide (a GIP + GLP-1 + glucagon **triple-receptor agonist**) is in Phase 3 and has **NO FDA-approved label and NO approved indication, starting dose, titration schedule, or marketed product.** Nothing below is approved prescribing guidance. Outside a clinical trial or other lawful authorized access, **retatrutide should not be prescribed.** All parameters are **derived from the published Phase 2 trial protocol** and are marked **VERIFY / investigational.** Confirm against the trial protocol (NCT04881760) and, once available, the eventual FDA label and Phase 3 (TRIUMPH program) data.

### 1. Candidacy / indication — **VERIFY (investigational)**
- **No approved indication.** Phase 2 enrolled adults with **BMI ≥30**, OR **BMI 27 to <30 plus ≥1 weight-related condition** (mirroring the obesity-drug paradigm). On-label vs off-label is **not applicable** — there is no label. Use only under an authorized clinical trial / IND.

### 2. Starting dose — **VERIFY (investigational)**
- In Phase 2, **initial doses of 2 mg or 4 mg SC once weekly** were studied; the **lower 2 mg start partially mitigated GI adverse events.** No approved starting dose exists. **VERIFY.**

### 3. Titration schedule — **VERIFY (investigational)**
- Phase 2 used weekly SC dosing with stepwise escalation toward target doses of **1, 4, 8, or 12 mg** (initial dose 2 or 4 mg, escalated over the early weeks). A **maximum studied dose of 12 mg weekly** produced the largest weight loss; an approved maximum is **not established.**
- **HOLD / SLOW principle (investigational, by analogy to the class and Phase 2 findings):** GI events were dose-related and reduced by a lower start and slower escalation — so **stay at the current dose / slow escalation for GI intolerance; do not skip steps.** Exact intervals/steps must follow the **trial protocol — VERIFY.**

### 4. Adverse events to watch — **VERIFY (investigational)**
- **COMMON (Phase 2):** gastrointestinal events were the most common, **dose-related, mostly mild–moderate**, and partially mitigated by the lower (2 mg) start — nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation (exact per-symptom percentages are dose-stratified in the trial; **VERIFY specific rates in the publication** rather than quoting a single number).
- **Notable signal:** **dose-dependent increases in heart rate**, peaking around week 24 and declining thereafter — monitor HR.
- **SERIOUS (anticipated by class; not a label list):** pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, AKI from volume depletion, hypoglycemia with insulin/SU, possible diabetic retinopathy effects, and the **class thyroid C-cell/MTC concern** are all **presumed** pending Phase 3/label characterization. **VERIFY.**

### 5. Side-effect MANAGEMENT — **VERIFY (investigational, by analogy to class)**
- **Nausea/vomiting:** dietary measures, hydration, **slow/hold escalation**, antiemetic for breakthrough — same approach as approved GLP-1 agents.
- **Constipation:** fiber, fluids, osmotic laxative.
- **Hypoglycemia:** reduce insulin/SU at initiation; monitor glucose.
- **Heart rate:** monitor for tachycardia given the observed dose-dependent HR rise.
- **Gallbladder / suspected pancreatitis:** evaluate biliary symptoms; **STOP and evaluate** for suspected pancreatitis and **do not restart if confirmed.**
- **Dehydration/AKI:** hold, hydrate, check renal function with significant GI losses.
- All management above is **extrapolated** and must follow the **trial protocol / safety plan — VERIFY.**

### 6. Monitoring / follow-up schedule — **VERIFY (investigational)**
- Per protocol/standard GLP-1 follow-up cadence and **CONTINUOUS (no cycling/rest)**: **baseline**, **~4 weeks**, **3 months**, **6 months**, then ongoing — checking weight/BMI, GI tolerability, BP, **heart rate (emphasized given the HR signal)**, renal function, glucose if dysglycemic, and mood. Trial protocols add study-specific labs/ECG/vitals — **follow the protocol. VERIFY.**

### 7. Discontinuation & what to expect — **VERIFY (investigational)**
- Long-term discontinuation/withdrawal data are **not yet established** (awaiting Phase 3). By class analogy, **weight regain after stopping is expected.** No taper guidance exists; the early up-titration is for GI tolerability. **VERIFY against eventual label/Phase 3 data.**

**Sources — Retatrutide (INVESTIGATIONAL):** **No FDA label exists — VERIFY.** Pivotal published evidence: Jastreboff AM, et al. *Triple-Hormone-Receptor Agonist Retatrutide for Obesity — A Phase 2 Trial*, N Engl J Med 2023;389:514-526 — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2301972) (PubMed PMID 37366315; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04881760). Phase 3 TRIUMPH program data and any future FDA label must be checked when available — **VERIFY.**
