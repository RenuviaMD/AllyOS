**Honesty posture:** Only FDA-approved agents (GLP-1s, Tesamorelin, Bremelanotide/PT-141, Elamipretide/FORZINITY) have *label-established* contraindications. **Curation flags raised by the research (need your decision):**

# Metabolic & Mitochondrial Peptides — Contraindications (AllyOS clinical decision-support)

_For LICENSED providers. Curated, not marketing. Compiled 2026-06-23._
_FDA-label items reflect approved-agent labeling. Research/compounded peptides have **no formally established contraindication list**; class/mechanism-based cautions are marked **VERIFY** and require independent clinician judgment._

### Semaglutide
**Absolute:**
- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) — boxed warning
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2) — boxed warning
- Prior serious hypersensitivity reaction (anaphylaxis/angioedema) to semaglutide or excipients
- Pregnancy — discontinue when pregnancy is recognized (may cause fetal harm)

**Relative:**
- History of pancreatitis — discontinue if pancreatitis suspected; do not restart if confirmed
- Severe gastroparesis / pre-existing severe GI motility disease (delayed gastric emptying)
- Active proliferative or non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy — monitor (rapid glycemic improvement linked to worsening; STEP/SUSTAIN-6 signal)
- History of cholelithiasis / gallbladder disease (cholelithiasis, cholecystitis reported)
- Risk of dehydration/acute kidney injury via GI losses — caution in CKD/volume depletion
- Concomitant insulin or insulin secretagogues (sulfonylureas) — hypoglycemia; reduce dose
- Lactation — limited data; weigh benefit/risk
- Severe hepatic impairment — limited data
- Procedural/anesthesia: retained gastric contents/aspiration risk; consider holding peri-procedure (ASA 2023 guidance)

_Basis: FDA Wegovy label (DailyMed setid ee06186f-2aa3-4990-a760-757579d8f77b; accessdata 215256s033lbl.pdf, 2026). Retinopathy signal: SUSTAIN-6, N Engl J Med 2016, DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1607141._

### SS-31 (Elamipretide)
**Now has an FDA label for ONE narrow indication — but NOT for the metabolic/mitochondrial-fatigue/healthspan use contemplated here.** Elamipretide (brand FORZINITY, Stealth BioTherapeutics) received FDA accelerated approval 2025-09-19 to improve muscle strength in adult and pediatric Barth syndrome patients weighing ≥30 kg. For any other (off-label/compounded longevity-metabolic) use, the contraindication framework is the FORZINITY label PLUS class/mechanism caution; off-label use is investigational.

**Absolute (from FORZINITY label):**
- Serious hypersensitivity to elamipretide or any excipient

**Relative / Warnings (FORZINITY label + precautionary):**
- Hypersensitivity reactions, including serious allergic reactions requiring emergency intervention — discontinue and seek care if immediate hypersensitivity occurs
- Not approved for intravenous use (subcutaneous only)
- Benzyl-alcohol excipient: NOT for neonates — fatal "gasping syndrome"/metabolic acidosis/neurotoxicity reported with BA-containing drugs in low-birth-weight (<2500 g) and preterm (<34 wk GA) neonates; avoid in pregnancy/neonates absent clear need
- Injection-site reactions (erythema, induration, pruritus, pain) are the most common AE (~15–20%) — expected, rarely dose-limiting
- Pregnancy — limited human data; no embryo-fetal toxicity in animals at 4–10× clinical AUC, but use only if clearly needed **VERIFY for off-label use**
- Lactation — no human data **VERIFY**
- Active malignancy (precautionary in compounded longevity protocols) — **VERIFY**; not a label contraindication
- Compounded/unregulated SS-31 (vs FORZINITY) — purity, identity, concentration, and benzyl-alcohol content unverified; empiric dosing **VERIFY**

_Basis: FDA FORZINITY label (DailyMed setid 146bf34c-76f2-48db-ac07-fb29cce2cd75; accessdata 215244s000lbl.pdf, 2025). Approval/mechanism: PubMed PMID 41260682. NOTE: label applies to Barth syndrome only; metabolic/longevity use is off-label and the malignancy and compounded-source cautions are VERIFY._

# === Cognitive / Longevity ===

# Contraindications — Cognitive / Neuro Peptides (adult clinical use)

**SCOPE & HONESTY NOTE (read first).** These are largely Russian-origin / research compounds. With the **sole partial exception of Cerebrolysin** (EU/RU/AR-marketed, real RCT data), **none are FDA-approved and NONE has a formally established, regulator-published contraindication list.** Items below are therefore a mix of (a) manufacturer labeling where a marketed product exists (Cerebrolysin only) and (b) **mechanism-based / prudent-practice precautions** that are *inferred, not validated* — these are marked **VERIFY**. "Absolute" for the non-approved compounds means "treat as a hard stop by convention/prudence," not "established in a trial." Do not present these as authoritative drug contraindications to patients. Source attribution: clinical-trial data via PubMed (DOIs inline); existing RenuviaMD protocol fields at `/home/user/renuviamd-site/protocols/protocols.json`.

### Cerebrolysin
**Absolute:**
- Known hypersensitivity to the product or excipients (porcine brain–derived peptide mixture; anaphylaxis reported) — *per manufacturer labeling.*
- Status epilepticus / active uncontrolled seizures (label warns against use during status epilepticus; rapid IV infusion can provoke seizures) — *per manufacturer labeling.* **VERIFY** against current local label.
- Severe renal impairment — *per manufacturer labeling.*
- Pregnancy and lactation (no adequate human data; label advises avoidance) — **VERIFY**.

**Relative (cautions):**
- Epilepsy / prior seizures or lowered seizure threshold — use, if at all, with slow infusion and monitoring (seizure-threshold mechanism). **VERIFY**.
- Concurrent serotonergic agents / MAO inhibitors — theoretical additive risk; space dosing. **VERIFY**.
- Allergic diathesis / atopy — infusion-related hypersensitivity and rare dizziness, flushing, palpitations, headache reported in trials.
- Renal function less than severe but impaired — dose caution. **VERIFY**.
- Counterfeit / unverified source (no US-approved product) — supply-authenticity caution (operational, not pharmacologic).

_Basis: CARS RCT (post-stroke) — "Cerebrolysin was safe and well tolerated," AE rate comparable to placebo, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1161/STROKEAHA.115.009416); AIS meta-analysis — neutral on mortality/SAE vs placebo, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1155/2017/4191670). Seizure-threshold, renal, hypersensitivity, pregnancy items = manufacturer labeling + mechanism, **VERIFY** against current local SmPC. According to PubMed._

### Semax

- Uncontrolled hypertension or significant cardiovascular disease — melanocortin/ACTH-axis and pressor theoretical effects. **VERIFY**.
- Nasal mucosal disease (for intranasal route) — local irritation. **VERIFY**.

_Basis: No regulator-published contraindications; no contraindication-defining clinical trials located. PubMed indexes ~229 Semax records, predominantly preclinical/mechanistic. All precautions are mechanism-based and marked **VERIFY**. According to PubMed._

### Selank
**Absolute:**
- **No formally established contraindication list exists** (not FDA-approved; Russian tuftsin-analog anxiolytic). Defensible hard stop: **known hypersensitivity to the peptide/diluent**.
- By prudent convention: **pregnancy, lactation, age <18** (no adequate human safety data). **VERIFY** — convention, not evidence.

**Relative (cautions):**

### DSIP (Delta sleep-inducing peptide)
**Absolute:**
- **No formally established contraindication list exists** (not FDA-approved; zero registered trials; older inconclusive human data — reviewers note DSIP does not reliably promote sleep). Defensible hard stop: **known hypersensitivity to the peptide/diluent**.
- By prudent convention: **pregnancy, lactation, age <18** (no adequate human safety data). **VERIFY** — convention, not evidence.

**Relative (cautions):**
- **Untreated obstructive sleep apnea / undiagnosed primary sleep disorder** — do not mask an untreated disorder with a sedating agent (clinical-practice caution; already flagged in RenuviaMD protocol). **VERIFY**.
- Concurrent sedatives / hypnotics / alcohol — theoretical additive CNS effect. **VERIFY**.
- Neuroendocrine/stress-axis modulation — DSIP affects ACTH/cortisol and neuroendocrine signaling in animal work; caution with endocrine instability. **VERIFY**.
- Variable inter-patient response — counsel that effect is unpredictable (not a safety contraindication but a consent point).

_Basis: No regulator-published contraindications; no contraindication-defining trials. Review: DSIP's link to sleep is "still unresolved," biological identity/receptor unconfirmed, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1471-4159.2006.03693.x). ### Epitalon (Epithalon)
**Absolute:**
- **No formally established contraindication list exists** (not FDA-approved; zero registered trials; telomere/longevity claims rest on in-vitro, animal, and weak older Russian reports, no Western replication). Defensible hard stop: **known hypersensitivity to the peptide/diluent**.
- **Active or prior malignancy** — treat as absolute by prudent convention. - Personal/strong family cancer history without current age-appropriate screening — gate on screening before use. **VERIFY**.
- Pineal/melatonin-axis or circadian disorders, and concurrent melatonin/hormonal therapy — theoretical additive melatonin-axis effect. **VERIFY**.
- Chronic continuous dosing — use short pulsed courses (10–20 days); chronic safety unknown. **VERIFY**.

_Basis: No regulator-published contraindications. Mechanism (pineal/melatonin-axis, proposed telomerase, mixed rodent carcinogenicity data) per Khavinson "Peptides and Ageing," [DOI not assigned; PMID 12374906]. Malignancy and all precautions are mechanism/precautionary, **VERIFY**. According to PubMed._

### PT-141 (Bremelanotide / Vyleesi)

FDA-approved (2019) for acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women. Statements below are from the Vyleesi label.

**Absolute:**
- Uncontrolled hypertension (label contraindication).
- Known cardiovascular disease (label contraindication).
- Hypersensitivity to bremelanotide or any component.

**Relative (cautions):**
- High cardiovascular risk — **not recommended**; assess CV risk before initiating and periodically, and ensure BP is well-controlled. Transient BP rise (≈ +6 mmHg SBP / +3 mmHg DBP, peak 2–4 h) with HR fall (up to −5 bpm); normalize within ~12 h.
- Dose limits to bound the BP effect: no more than 1 dose / 24 h and no more than 8 doses / month.
- Oral naltrexone-containing products — Vyleesi may significantly decrease naltrexone plasma levels; **not recommended** in patients on oral naltrexone for alcohol/opioid dependence (would lose its benefit). **VERIFY exact interaction wording against current label.**
- Nausea — most common adverse reaction; can prompt antiemetic / discontinuation.
- Focal hyperpigmentation (face, gingiva, breasts) — more frequent with daily dosing and in darker skin types; may not fully resolve on discontinuation.
- Severe hepatic or renal impairment — use caution / higher systemic exposure; **VERIFY specific-population labeling.**
- Pregnancy — discontinue if pregnancy suspected (indication is non-pregnant premenopausal women).

_Basis: FDA Vyleesi (bremelanotide) Prescribing Information — DailyMed setid 8c9607a2-5b57-4a59-b159-cf196deebdd9 (https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=8c9607a2-5b57-4a59-b159-cf196deebdd9) and FDA label PDF (https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2019/210557s000lbl.pdf). ### Kisspeptin-10

Investigational only — no FDA approval and no validated label. All items are mechanism-based (KISS1R agonist → endogenous GnRH → LH/FSH) from the clinical-research literature.

**Absolute (mechanism-based — VERIFY):**
- Hormone-sensitive malignancy (breast, prostate, endometrial, ovarian) — upstream stimulation of sex-hormone signaling; precautionary absolute. **VERIFY**

**Relative (cautions — VERIFY):**

_Basis: Mechanism (KISS1R/GnRH) + investigational human data. Comninos/Dhillo HSDD RCT, JAMA Netw Open 2023, PMC9898824 (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9898824/). No FDA label exists — all contraindications are mechanism-based and marked VERIFY._

### Gonadorelin

Synthetic GnRH (decapeptide). Brand products (Factrel, Lutrepulse) historically FDA-approved but discontinued; only US availability is compounded. Off-label for male HPG-axis support during TRT and female ovulation induction. Existing protocols.json entry: "hormone-sensitive malignancy, pregnancy (unless ovulation-induction protocol), pituitary adenoma, hypersensitivity."

**Absolute:**
- Hypersensitivity to gonadorelin or any component (anaphylaxis and hypersensitivity reactions — bronchospasm, tachycardia, flushing, urticaria, injection-site induration — reported with multiple-dose use) — label contraindication.
- Any condition that could be aggravated by an increase in gonadotropins/sex steroids (e.g., hormone-sensitive malignancy) — precautionary absolute.
- Pituitary adenoma / pituitary tumor (existing library entry) — **VERIFY against label; treat as precaution.**

**Relative (cautions):**
- Pregnancy — use only if clearly needed; fetal effects of LH-RH not adequately evaluated (acceptable within a supervised ovulation-induction protocol).
- Pre-existing ovarian enlargement / risk of ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) — avoid; risk of cyst rupture, ascites, thromboembolism; avoid within ~3 months of a severe hyperstimulation event.
- Pulsatile physiology matters — non-pulsatile/continuous exposure downregulates the axis (the basis of GnRH-agonist suppression); dosing route/cadence is clinically load-bearing.
- Source/compounding verification (no marketed FDA-approved product).

_Basis: FDA Factrel (gonadorelin) labeling — Contraindications/Warnings (hypersensitivity, pregnancy, ovarian enlargement/OHSS); RxList Factrel monograph (https://www.rxlist.com/factrel-drug.htm). Pituitary-adenoma item from existing protocols.json — marked VERIFY. Pulsatile ovulation-induction context: Gynecol Endocrinol 2026, https://doi.org/10.1080/09513590.2026.2677994._

### Melanotan II

NOT FDA-approved. Non-selective melanocortin agonist (MC1R–MC5R). All items are mechanism-based and derived from published case reports / toxicology series.

**Absolute (mechanism-based — VERIFY):**
- Melanoma or any pigmented/atypical/changing skin lesion (and personal history of melanoma) — melanocortin stimulation of melanocyte proliferation; documented melanoma and changing-mole case reports, including oral mucosal melanoma after intranasal use. **VERIFY**
- Uncontrolled hypertension or significant cardiovascular disease — documented hypertensive crises (SBP > 180 in majority of toxicity-series cases) and a case of renal infarction. **VERIFY**
- Known hypersensitivity to the peptide/diluent.

**Relative (cautions — mechanism-based — VERIFY):**
- Priapism risk (melanocortin erectogenic effect) — counsel/avoid in men predisposed (sickle cell, prior priapism, anatomic risk); priapism > 4 h reported in case series and is a urologic emergency. **VERIFY**
- Severe nausea/vomiting — very common in case reports; rhabdomyolysis (CK > 5,000) and systemic toxicity reported. **VERIFY**
- Numerous/dysplastic nevi or family history of melanoma — heightened pigmented-lesion surveillance concern; baseline + interval dermatologic exam. **VERIFY**
- Pregnancy, lactation, age < 18 — precautionary (no safety data). **VERIFY**
- Unregulated/grey-market supply — purity and dosing unverified; major safety contributor.

_Basis: Mechanism (non-selective melanocortin agonist). Published case reports/series — priapism overdose (Clin Toxicol 2013, https://doi.org/10.3109/15563650.2013.784775); systemic toxicity/rhabdomyolysis (Clin Toxicol case report); melanoma/changing-nevi case reports. No FDA label exists — all items mechanism-based and marked VERIFY._
