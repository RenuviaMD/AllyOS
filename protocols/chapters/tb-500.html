Thymosin beta-4 (TB-500)

Soft-tissue/tendon injury and muscle recovery, adjunct to rehab
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade D (CONSUMER FRAGMENT)
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk
FDA PCAC — 2026-07-23. Under PCAC review for the 503A compounding list — non-binding, not FDA approval.

Mechanism

Actin-binding peptide — promotes cell migration, angiogenesis, and tissue regeneration

Clinical notes

Pairs with BPC-157 for the classic repair stack. Same angiogenic cancer caution applies. Loading phase twice weekly, then taper to maintenance.

Indication

Adjunct for soft-tissue, tendon, or muscle injury and recovery where standard rehab has plateaued.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active or prior malignancy (pro-angiogenic — absolute)
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity to peptide/diluent
  • Undiagnosed mass / malignancy red flag

Rx

  • Dose: 2–2.5 mg SC twice weekly (loading ×4–6 wk), then 2 mg weekly maintenance
  • Route: SC
  • Reconstitution: 10 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 5 mg/mL = 5,000 mcg/mL → 2 mg = 0.40 mL = 40 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 6 weeks loading + 4–6 weeks maintenance
  • Stack pairs: bpc-157, ghk-cu

Labs (baseline)

  • CBC, CMP
  • Age-appropriate cancer screening current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; human evidence is preclinical/anecdotal.
  • Tell us any cancer history — theoretical tumor-growth risk.
  • An adjunct to rehab, not a replacement for it.
  • Report any new mass or unexplained symptom promptly.

Evidence

Full-length Tβ4 (RGN-259) has completed Phase 3 ophthalmic RCTs; the **consumer fragment has no completed human efficacy trial** (NCT07487363, Ph1/2, recruiting). PubMed coverage of literal "TB-500" is mostly doping analytics.
