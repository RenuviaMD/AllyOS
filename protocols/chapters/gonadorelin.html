Gonadorelin

Historically FDA-approved (brands discontinued); compounded use for male HPG-axis support during TRT and female ovulation induction
FDA StatusOff-label
Evidence GradeGrade C
ClassificationPhysician-review
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Synthetic GnRH - stimulates pituitary LH/FSH release (pulsatile physiology)

Clinical notes

Grade C (B for female pulsatile ovulation induction). Brand products discontinued; only US availability is compounded (reportedly Category 1). Male TRT/fertility use is off-label with limited modern evidence. Pulsatile delivery matters physiologically.

Indication

Historically approved for diagnostic/ovulation-induction use (brands discontinued). Compounded off-label use for HPG-axis support during TRT and female ovulation induction.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Hormone-sensitive malignancy
  • Pituitary adenoma
  • Pregnancy (unless ovulation-induction protocol)
  • Hypersensitivity

Rx

  • Dose: indication- and protocol-specific; pulsatile dosing for ovulation induction. Male TRT-adjunct dosing is off-label and not standardized.
  • Route: SC / IV (pulsatile)
  • Cycle: protocol-dependent

Labs (baseline)

  • LH, FSH, testosterone (men) / estradiol + cycle monitoring (women); pregnancy status

Safety & compliance gate

  • Contraindications excluded; honest evidence-grade (C) disclosed
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Enhanced/experimental informed consent where investigational

Patient education key points

  • Evidence grade C; status reviewed and signed by the treating physician.
  • Report unexpected symptoms; source purity may be unregulated for non-approved peptides.

Evidence

Brand GnRH products (Factrel/Lutrepulse) discontinued; only US availability is compounded. Female pulsatile ovulation-induction has real evidence; male TRT-adjunct use is off-label with limited modern data.
