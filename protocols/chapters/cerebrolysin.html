Cerebrolysin

Cognitive support in post-stroke, TBI, and neurodegenerative decline
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade B/C
ClassificationPhysician-review
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Porcine brain-derived neuropeptide mix — neurotrophic (BDNF/NGF-like) and neuroprotective activity

Clinical notes

EU/RU/AR-labeled, no US brand — fully off-shore. Given as IV/IM courses, not a quick injection. Epilepsy is a caution. Verify a legitimate source — counterfeits exist.

Indication

Cognitive support in post-stroke, TBI, or neurodegenerative decline. <!-- NOTE: EU/RU/AR-labeled; not FDA-approved -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Severe renal impairment
  • Status epilepticus / uncontrolled epilepsy

Rx

  • Dose: 5–10 mL daily ×10–20 day course
  • Route: IM (≤5 mL) or slow IV (larger volumes)
  • Reconstitution: Pre-mixed ampoule (215.2 mg/mL solution) — no reconstitution
  • Cycle: 10–20 day course, repeat 2–4×/year
  • Stack pairs: semax, selank, dsip

Labs (baseline)

  • CMP (renal), CBC
  • Seizure/neuro history review

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label/investigational consent signed (`consent_investigational_peptide`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_cerebrolysin`)
  • Source pharmacy verified (counterfeit risk)
  • Renal function + seizure history documented

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved in the US; used as IV/IM courses.
  • Porcine-derived — tell us about allergies.
  • Report any seizure activity or dizziness.
  • Source authenticity matters — we verify supply.

Evidence

42 trials + meta-analyses, **mixed**: favorable Alzheimer's meta-analysis + positive post-stroke RCT (CARS), but a **stroke meta-analysis found no significant efficacy.** Approved in several non-US countries.

References

  • Muresanu 2016, Stroke (CARS)
  • Gauthier 2015, Dement Geriatr Cogn Disord (AD meta)
  • Zhang 2017, Biomed Res Int (negative stroke meta)
