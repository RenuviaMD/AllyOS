Tesamorelin

Excess visceral adiposity (on-label: HIV lipodystrophy); off-label VAT reduction
FDA StatusFDA-approved
Evidence GradeGrade A (LABEL INDICATION)
ClassificationFDA-approved pathway
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Stabilized GHRH analog — stimulates GH release, reduces visceral adipose tissue

Clinical notes

Strongest GH-secretagogue data for visceral fat. Brand is pricey; compounded common but verify source. Monitor IGF-1 and glucose — it can nudge insulin resistance.

Indication

Reduction of excess visceral adipose tissue — on-label in HIV-associated lipodystrophy; off-label for VAT reduction. <!-- NOTE: brand Egrifta substantially more expensive than compounded; pricing reflects compounded tier -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active malignancy (absolute)
  • Untreated severe hypothyroidism
  • Pregnancy / disrupted HPA axis
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity to tesamorelin/mannitol

Rx

  • Dose: 2 mg SC daily
  • Route: SC (abdomen), rotate
  • Reconstitution: 2 mg vial + 2.1 mL sterile water; inject 2 mL ≈ 2 mg. <!-- NOTE: per Egrifta SV labeling -->
  • Cycle: 12–26 weeks; reassess VAT
  • Stack pairs: cjc-ipamorelin-blend, aod-9604, mots-c

Labs (baseline)

  • IGF-1, TSH/free T4 ← gating
  • Fasting glucose/A1c, lipids, CMP

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label consent signed (`consent_gh_secretagogue`) — N/A if on-label

  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Thyroid treated + cancer screen current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • FDA-approved for HIV-related belly fat; other uses are off-label.
  • Daily injection; may affect blood sugar — we monitor.
  • Report swelling, joint pain, or tingling.
  • Tell us any cancer history.

Evidence

FDA-approved (2010) for HIV-associated lipodystrophy. Two pivotal Phase 3 RCTs (pooled VAT −15.4% vs placebo, glucose-neutral) + ongoing MASLD/liver trials. Off-label for general "anti-aging."
