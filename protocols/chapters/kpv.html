KPV

Anti-inflammatory support, gut/skin inflammation adjunct
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade D
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk
FDA PCAC — 2026-07-23. Under PCAC review for the 503A compounding list — non-binding, not FDA approval.

Mechanism

α-MSH C-terminal tripeptide — anti-inflammatory via NF-κB downregulation

Clinical notes

Anti-inflammatory tripeptide, oral or SC. Useful gut/skin adjunct; preclinical evidence. Pairs with Tα1 for an immune-axis combo. Not a substitute for IBD work-up.

Indication

Anti-inflammatory support, particularly as a gut or skin inflammation adjunct. <!-- NOTE: preclinical evidence; no human RCTs -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Transplant on immunosuppression (precautionary)
  • Severe active autoimmune disease
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity

Rx

  • Dose: 200–500 mcg SC daily (or oral capsule)
  • Route: SC or oral
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2.5 mL BAC water = 2 mg/mL = 2,000 mcg/mL → 500 mcg = 0.25 mL = 25 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 4–8 weeks
  • Stack pairs: thymosin-alpha-1, bpc-157

Labs (baseline)

  • CBC, CMP, CRP/ESR if inflammatory
  • GI/derm work-up as indicated

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; evidence is preclinical.
  • An adjunct — doesn't replace work-up of gut/skin disease.
  • Tell us about autoimmune conditions or immune-suppressing meds.
  • Report any unusual symptoms.

Evidence

**Preclinical only — zero human trials of any phase** (mouse colitis/IBD + in-vitro).
