Delta sleep-inducing peptide (DSIP)

Sleep-onset/quality support, adjunct in stress-related insomnia
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade D
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk
FDA PCAC — 2026-07-24. Under PCAC review for the 503A compounding list — non-binding, not FDA approval.

Mechanism

Nonapeptide — modulates sleep architecture and stress/neuroendocrine response

Clinical notes

Inconsistent human data; effects are variable patient-to-patient. Bedtime SC dosing. Address sleep hygiene and OSA first — don't paper over an untreated disorder.

Indication

Sleep-onset/quality support as an adjunct in stress-related insomnia. <!-- NOTE: human evidence inconsistent -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Untreated OSA / primary sleep disorder

Rx

  • Dose: 100–300 mcg SC at bedtime
  • Route: SC
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 2.5 mg/mL = 2,500 mcg/mL → 200 mcg = 0.08 mL = 8 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 4–6 weeks
  • Stack pairs: selank, cerebrolysin

Labs (baseline)

  • Generally none required
  • Sleep history / OSA screen

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; effects vary person to person.
  • We address sleep hygiene and screen for sleep apnea first.
  • Inject at bedtime as directed.
  • Report daytime grogginess or unusual symptoms.

Evidence

**Zero registered trials.** Old, sparse, inconclusive human data; reviewers note DSIP does not reliably promote sleep. Modern data preclinical.

References
