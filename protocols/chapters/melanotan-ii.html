Melanotan II

Reported (gray-market) for skin tanning and sexual arousal — not approved for any use
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade D
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Non-selective melanocortin receptor agonist (MC1R/MC3R/MC4R) — stimulates melanogenesis (tanning) with central effects on libido and appetite

Clinical notes

Grade D, not approved. Major safety concern — case reports of melanoma and new/darkening or changing moles after use; nausea, flushing, and priapism are common. Distinct from FDA-approved afamelanotide (SCENESSE). Mandate full-body skin surveillance; many clinicians decline to prescribe.

Indication

No approved indication. Reported marketplace use for skin tanning and for sexual arousal/libido. Included as a reference entry because of high consumer demand — not a recommendation to prescribe.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Personal or family history of melanoma (absolute)
  • Atypical / dysplastic nevi or dysplastic nevus syndrome
  • Pregnancy / lactation
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Uncontrolled hypertension / cardiovascular instability
  • Unverified / gray-market source

Rx

  • Dose: *reported* (marketplace; no validated regimen): low microgram SC dosing with cautious titration. Not a recommended protocol.
  • Route: SC
  • Reconstitution: per product (gray-market potency/purity unreliable — forensic analyses have found ~30% purity)
  • Cycle: N/A — research/gray-market
  • Stack pairs: none recommended

Labs (baseline)

  • Full skin/mole mapping and dermatologic baseline; BP; CMP

Safety & compliance gate

  • Melanoma / atypical-nevi history excluded (absolute)
  • Dermatologic surveillance plan documented
  • Patient counseled on melanoma case reports + unregulated purity
  • Enhanced/experimental informed consent obtained

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; not the same as afamelanotide (SCENESSE).
  • Case reports link use to melanoma and to new/changing moles — any pigmented-lesion change requires urgent evaluation.
  • Common effects: nausea, facial flushing, spontaneous erections (priapism).
  • Source purity is unregulated and unreliable.

Evidence

No FDA approval; small/early efficacy data plus harm signals — case reports of melanoma and mucosal hyperpigmentation after use; nausea/flushing/priapism common. Distinct from FDA-approved afamelanotide.
