Thymosin alpha-1

Immune modulation in chronic/recurrent infection or immunosenescence
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade B–C
ClassificationPhysician-review
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

28-aa thymic peptide — TLR-2/9-mediated T-cell maturation and Th1 immune modulation

Clinical notes

Modulator, not a generic booster — use against a defined immune problem. Always ask about transplant/immunosuppression. Strongest data is in hepatitis; favorable safety profile.

Indication

Immune modulation in chronic/recurrent infection or immunosenescence. <!-- NOTE: Zadaxin approved in ~35 countries; not FDA-approved in US -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Transplant on immunosuppression (rejection risk — absolute)
  • Severe active autoimmune disease
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity

Rx

  • Dose: 1.6 mg SC twice weekly
  • Route: SC
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 1 mL BAC water = 5 mg/mL = 5,000 mcg/mL → 1.6 mg = 0.32 mL = 32 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 8–24 weeks (indication-driven)
  • Stack pairs: kpv

Labs (baseline)

  • CBC w/ diff (± lymphocyte subsets), CMP/LFTs
  • Autoimmune screen if history; viral serologies as indicated
  • Transplant/immunosuppression status ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved in the US; modulates immune tone.
  • Tell us about any transplant, immune-suppressing meds, or autoimmune disease.
  • Report new joint pain, rash, or fatigue (possible flare).
  • Generally well tolerated; report injection-site reactions.

Evidence

Real RCTs — chronic hepatitis B (NCT00291616, NCT02366208), melanoma adjuvant (NCT00911443), pancreatitis (TRACE), colorectal adjuvant (NCT05086614 recruiting). Approved as Zadaxin abroad; not FDA-approved. Robust for *specific* indications, **not** "vitality/anti-aging."
