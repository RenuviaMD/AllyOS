AOD-9604

Adjunct fat-loss support in metabolic/weight programs
FDA StatusInvestigational
Evidence GradeGrade D
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

GH fragment (176-191) — stimulates lipolysis without affecting blood glucose or IGF-1

Clinical notes

Mild effect, clean metabolic profile — no IGF-1 or glucose impact. Best as an adjunct, not a standalone. Human weight-loss data is weak; set expectations low.

Indication

Adjunct fat-loss support within a structured metabolic/weight program. <!-- NOTE: human efficacy data weak; modest at best -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity to peptide/diluent
  • Active malignancy (precautionary)

Rx

  • Dose: 300 mcg SC daily; range 250–500 mcg/day
  • Route: SC (fasted/AM)
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 2.5 mg/mL = 2,500 mcg/mL → 300 mcg = 0.12 mL = 12 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 8–12 weeks
  • Stack pairs: tesamorelin, cjc-ipamorelin-blend, mots-c

Labs (baseline)

  • CMP, fasting glucose, lipids, weight/waist

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; effects are modest and adjunctive.
  • Does not replace diet and exercise — those drive results.
  • Doesn't affect blood sugar or IGF-1.
  • Report injection-site reactions or unexpected symptoms.

Evidence

Phase 2 obesity development **discontinued for lack of meaningful efficacy**; **zero registered trials**; substantive data preclinical.
