Ipamorelin

GH-axis support for recovery, body composition, and sleep, paired with a GHRH analog
FDA StatusInvestigational
Evidence GradeGrade C/D
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Selective ghrelin/GHSR agonist — stimulates GH pulse with minimal cortisol/prolactin effect

Clinical notes

Cleanest GHRP — minimal hunger/cortisol vs GHRP-6. Synergistic with CJC-1295 or sermorelin. Bedtime dosing leverages the natural GH pulse.

Indication

GH-axis support for recovery, body composition, and sleep quality, typically combined with a GHRH analog.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active malignancy (absolute)
  • Untreated severe hypothyroidism
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity

Rx

  • Dose: 200–300 mcg SC nightly (or BID)
  • Route: SC, bedtime
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2.5 mL BAC water = 2 mg/mL = 2,000 mcg/mL → 300 mcg = 0.15 mL = 15 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 12–16 weeks
  • Stack pairs: cjc-1295, sermorelin

Labs (baseline)

  • IGF-1, TSH/free T4 ← gating
  • Fasting glucose, CMP, lipids

Safety & compliance gate

  • Investigational consent signed (`consent_gh_secretagogue`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_ipamorelin`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Thyroid treated + cancer screen current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; compounded GH secretagogue.
  • Bedtime, empty stomach for best effect.
  • Report swelling, joint pain, or tingling.
  • We monitor IGF-1 and blood sugar over time.

Evidence

Two completed human Phase 2 trials (GI motility) — **both negative** (NCT00672074 missed primary endpoint; NCT01280344). Zero human efficacy data for anti-aging/muscle/fat use.

References
