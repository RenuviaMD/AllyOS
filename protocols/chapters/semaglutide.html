Semaglutide

Type 2 diabetes; chronic weight management; MASH with fibrosis (2025); CV and renal risk reduction
FDA StatusFDA-approved
Evidence GradeGrade A
ClassificationFDA-approved pathway
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

GLP-1 receptor agonist — glucose-dependent insulin secretion, slowed gastric emptying, central appetite suppression

Clinical notes

FDA-approved, Grade A — the reference GLP-1. Titrate slowly to limit GI effects. Now approved for MASH (2025) with positive renal (FLOW) and CV (SELECT) outcomes. Counsel on reduced oral-contraceptive reliability with delayed gastric emptying, and on pancreatitis/gallbladder symptoms.

Indication

Type 2 diabetes; chronic weight management (BMI ≥30, or ≥27 with a weight-related comorbidity); MASH with moderate–advanced fibrosis (Wegovy, 2025); cardiovascular risk reduction; renal protection in T2D with CKD.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2 (boxed warning)
  • Pregnancy or planning pregnancy
  • Prior serious hypersensitivity to semaglutide
  • History of pancreatitis (caution)
  • Severe gastroparesis
  • Active proliferative diabetic retinopathy (caution — monitor)

Rx

  • Dose (label, indication-specific): Wegovy SC weekly 0.25 → 0.5 → 1.0 → 1.7 → 2.4 mg (escalate ~q4wk). Ozempic SC weekly 0.25 → 0.5 → 1.0 (→2.0) mg. Rybelsus oral 3 mg ×30d → 7 mg → 14 mg daily (fasting, plain water).
  • Route: SC once weekly (or oral daily — Rybelsus)
  • Titration: increase no faster than every 4 weeks to limit nausea/GI effects
  • Cycle: chronic, indication-dependent
  • Stack pairs: per prescriber judgment

Labs (baseline)

  • A1c, fasting glucose, CMP, lipids, weight/BMI; pregnancy status; lipase if pancreatitis risk

Safety & compliance gate

  • MTC/MEN2 excluded; pancreatitis risk addressed
  • Contraception counseling documented (delayed gastric emptying)
  • Patient education delivered (`patient-semaglutide`)
  • Source pharmacy verified

Patient education key points

  • FDA-approved; on-label use per prescriber judgment.
  • Inject the same day each week; rotate sites.
  • Report severe abdominal pain (pancreatitis), gallbladder symptoms, or persistent vomiting.
  • Weight/glucose effects regress if discontinued.

Evidence

Extensive Phase 3 program (SUSTAIN/STEP). FDA-approved for T2D, chronic weight management, and (2025) MASH with fibrosis; FLOW renal and SELECT cardiovascular outcomes positive.
