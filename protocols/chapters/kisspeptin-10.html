Kisspeptin-10

Libido and reproductive-axis support; investigational
FDA StatusInvestigational
Evidence GradeGrade C
ClassificationPhysician-review
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

KISS1R agonist — stimulates hypothalamic GnRH release, upstream of LH/FSH

Clinical notes

Very early human data, mostly IV research dosing — SC use is empiric. Acts upstream on GnRH. Caution with hormone-sensitive conditions; consent as experimental.

Indication

Libido and reproductive-axis support. <!-- NOTE: investigational; human data mostly IV research dosing, SC empiric -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active hormone-sensitive malignancy (absolute)
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation
  • Age <18
  • Hypersensitivity

Rx

  • Dose: ~100 mcg SC (empiric); range narrow, start low <!-- NOTE: no validated SC regimen -->
  • Route: SC
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2.5 mL BAC water = 2 mg/mL = 2,000 mcg/mL → 100 mcg = 0.05 mL = 5 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 4–8 weeks
  • Stack pairs: pt-141

Labs (baseline)

  • LH/FSH, total testosterone/estradiol, prolactin
  • Hormone-sensitive cancer screen

Safety & compliance gate

  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_kisspeptin-10`)

  • Hormone-sensitive cancer screen documented

Patient education key points

  • Experimental; very limited human data.
  • Acts on the brain's hormone-control center.
  • Tell us any hormone-sensitive cancer history.
  • Report unusual symptoms; effects may be subtle.

Evidence

Genuine human Phase 1 physiology studies (reliably stimulates LH acutely) — NCT00914823, NCT03286517 — but **no completed efficacy RCTs** for libido/fertility/"vitality."
