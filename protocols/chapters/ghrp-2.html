GHRP-2 (Pralmorelin)

GH-stimulation diagnostic agent abroad; reported off-label for GH elevation - not approved in the US
FDA StatusResearch-only
Evidence GradeGrade D
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Synthetic GH-releasing peptide / ghrelin-receptor agonist - stimulates pituitary GH release

Clinical notes

Grade D therapeutic. Recognized GH-stimulation test agent (approved in some countries) but no US therapeutic approval and no efficacy trials for anti-aging/body-composition use. Often stacked with a GHRH analog (unproven).

Indication

No US therapeutic approval. Diagnostic GH-stimulation use abroad; reported off-label for GH elevation. Reference entry only.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active malignancy
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Caution: appetite/cortisol/prolactin effects

Rx

  • Dose: *reported* (marketplace): ~100-300 mcg SC 1-3x/day, often stacked with a GHRH analog.
  • Route: SC
  • Cycle: no validated protocol

Labs (baseline)

  • IGF-1, TSH/free T4, fasting glucose/A1c

Safety & compliance gate

  • Contraindications excluded; honest evidence-grade (D) disclosed
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Enhanced/experimental informed consent where investigational

Patient education key points

  • Evidence grade D; status reviewed and signed by the treating physician.
  • Report unexpected symptoms; source purity may be unregulated for non-approved peptides.

Evidence

GH-stimulation diagnostic agent abroad; no US therapeutic approval and no efficacy trials for wellness/anti-aging use. 2024–26 literature is diagnostic only.
