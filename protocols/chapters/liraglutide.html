Liraglutide

Type 2 diabetes (Victoza) and chronic weight management (Saxenda); pediatric weight data
FDA StatusFDA-approved
Evidence GradeGrade A
ClassificationFDA-approved pathway
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

GLP-1 receptor agonist (once-daily) — insulin secretion, slowed gastric emptying, appetite suppression

Clinical notes

FDA-approved, Grade A. Daily dosing (vs weekly semaglutide). First generic Victoza (2024) and generic Saxenda (2025) now available; SCALE Kids extended pediatric use. Titrate to limit GI effects.

Indication

Type 2 diabetes (Victoza); chronic weight management, BMI >=30 or >=27 with comorbidity (Saxenda); pediatric obesity per label.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Personal/family history of MTC or MEN2 (boxed)
  • Pregnancy
  • History of pancreatitis (caution)
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Severe gastroparesis

Rx

  • Dose: Victoza 0.6 mg SC daily x1wk -> 1.2 -> 1.8 mg. Saxenda 0.6 mg daily, +0.6 mg weekly -> 3.0 mg.
  • Route: SC once daily
  • Titration: weekly steps to limit nausea
  • Cycle: chronic

Labs (baseline)

  • A1c, fasting glucose, CMP, lipids, weight/BMI; pregnancy status; lipase if pancreatitis risk

Safety & compliance gate

  • Contraindications excluded; honest evidence-grade (A) disclosed
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Enhanced/experimental informed consent where investigational

Patient education key points

  • Evidence grade A; status reviewed and signed by the treating physician.
  • Report unexpected symptoms; source purity may be unregulated for non-approved peptides.

Evidence

FDA-approved (Victoza for T2D, Saxenda for weight). SCALE Kids extended pediatric data; first generics approved 2024–2025.
