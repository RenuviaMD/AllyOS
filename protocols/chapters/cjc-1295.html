CJC-1295

GH-axis support paired with a ghrelin agonist for recovery and body composition
FDA StatusInvestigational
Evidence GradeGrade C (DAC) / D (NO-DAC)
ClassificationResearch-only / exclusion
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

Long-acting GHRH analog — sustained stimulation of pituitary GH release

Clinical notes

Default here is no-DAC (mod GRF 1-29), dosed daily; the DAC version is weekly. Almost always paired with ipamorelin. Watch fluid retention at higher doses.

Indication

GH-axis support for recovery and body composition, paired with a ghrelin agonist. <!-- NOTE: card assumes no-DAC (mod GRF 1-29), daily; DAC version is 1-2 mg weekly -->

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active malignancy (absolute)
  • Untreated severe hypothyroidism
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity

Rx

  • Dose: 100 mcg SC nightly (no-DAC); range 100–200 mcg
  • Route: SC, bedtime
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 2.5 mL BAC water = 2 mg/mL = 2,000 mcg/mL → 100 mcg = 0.05 mL = 5 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 12–16 weeks
  • Stack pairs: ipamorelin, sermorelin

Labs (baseline)

  • IGF-1, TSH/free T4 ← gating
  • Fasting glucose, CMP, lipids

Safety & compliance gate

  • Investigational consent signed (`consent_gh_secretagogue`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_cjc-1295`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Thyroid treated + cancer screen current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Not FDA-approved; compounded GH secretagogue.
  • Bedtime, empty stomach.
  • Report swelling, joint aches, or tingling.
  • We track IGF-1 and glucose.

Evidence

One human PK/PD RCT of the DAC form (dose-dependent GH/IGF-1 rise, t½ ~6–8 days); Phase 2 NCT00267527 terminated. "No-DAC" borrows the DAC molecule's credibility.
