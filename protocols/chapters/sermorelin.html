Sermorelin

Adult age-related GH decline with low-normal IGF-1 and consistent symptoms
FDA StatusOff-label
Evidence GradeGrade B (PEDS/DX) / D (ADULT WELLNESS)
ClassificationPhysician-review
LiabilityPrescriber assumes full risk

Mechanism

GHRH(1-29) analog — stimulates endogenous pulsatile GH release via pituitary GHRH receptor

Clinical notes

Brand discontinued — compounded only. Treat thyroid first or you'll mislabel a responder. Bedtime, empty stomach; chase IGF-1 upper-normal-for-age, not above.

Indication

Adult symptomatic age-related GH decline with low-normal/low IGF-1 after excluding other causes.

Rule out (contraindications)

  • Active malignancy (absolute)
  • Untreated severe hypothyroidism (treat first)
  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation; age <18
  • Hypersensitivity
  • Non-functional pituitary (won't respond)

Rx

  • Dose: 300 mcg SC nightly; range 200–500 mcg
  • Route: SC, bedtime, empty stomach
  • Reconstitution: 5 mg + 5 mL BAC water = 1 mg/mL = 1,000 mcg/mL → 300 mcg = 0.30 mL = 30 units (U-100)
  • Cycle: 12–24 weeks; reassess
  • Stack pairs: ipamorelin, cjc-1295

Labs (baseline)

  • IGF-1, TSH/free T4 ← gating (thyroid + IGF-1)
  • Fasting glucose/A1c, CMP, lipids, CBC

Safety & compliance gate

  • Off-label consent signed (`consent_gh_secretagogue`)
  • Patient education delivered (`patiented_sermorelin`)
  • Source pharmacy verified
  • Thyroid treated + cancer screen current ← gating

Patient education key points

  • Compounded/off-label; this is not synthetic HGH.
  • Inject at bedtime on an empty stomach — no carbs first.
  • Benefits are modest; we track IGF-1 and symptoms.
  • Report swelling, joint aches, or tingling (signs of too much).

Evidence

Real FDA history — was approved (Geref) for pediatric GHD diagnosis/treatment, now discontinued; compounded use not FDA-approved. No RCTs for adult "anti-aging" use.
