# Metabolic Stack (Tesamorelin + CJC/Ipa Blend + AOD-9604 + MOTS-c)

## Indication
Visceral fat reduction and metabolic optimization in motivated patients with measured excess VAT.

## Rule out (contraindications — union)
- ☐ Active malignancy (absolute)
- ☐ Untreated severe hypothyroidism
- ☐ Pregnancy
- ☐ Lactation; age <18
- ☐ Hypersensitivity

## Rx
- **Dose:** Tesamorelin 2 mg daily / CJC-Ipa blend 0.15 mL nightly / AOD-9604 300 mcg daily / MOTS-c 5–10 mg 2–3×/wk
- **Route:** SC
- **Reconstitution:** per-component (Tesa 2 mL≈2 mg; blend 2 mg/mL→15u; AOD 2.5 mg/mL→12u; MOTS-c 10 mg/mL→50u)
- **Cycle:** 8–12 weeks
- **Components:** tesamorelin, cjc-ipamorelin-blend, aod-9604, mots-c

## Labs (baseline)
- **IGF-1, TSH/free T4, A1c/HOMA-IR ← gating**
- Lipids, CMP, waist/VAT

## Ongoing care
| When | What to check | What to adjust |
|---|---|---|
| Wk 2 | Tolerance, glucose | Continue |
| Mo 1 | Glucose, waist | Hold/adjust |
| Mo 3 | IGF-1, A1c, VAT | Titrate; keep IGF-1 in range |
| Mo 6 | VAT, A1c, lipids | Taper to maintenance |

## Safety & compliance gate
- ☐ Investigational consent signed (`consent_gh_secretagogue`)
- ☐ Patient education delivered (`patiented_metabolic-stack`)
- ☐ Source pharmacy verified
- ☐ Thyroid treated + cancer screen + baseline A1c ← gating

## Patient education key points
- Mostly not FDA-approved; an adjunct to diet and exercise.
- Several agents can affect blood sugar — we monitor A1c.
- Report swelling, joint pain, or tingling.
- Tell us any cancer history.
