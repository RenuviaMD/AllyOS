# Copper tripeptide-1 (GHK-Cu)

## Indication
Skin and connective-tissue repair, hair support, and aesthetic regeneration. <!-- NOTE: strongest data is topical; systemic SC dosing is empiric -->

## Rule out (contraindications)
- ☐ Active or prior malignancy (pro-angiogenic — absolute)
- ☐ Pregnancy
- ☐ Lactation
- ☐ Age <18
- ☐ Hypersensitivity / copper sensitivity
- ☐ Wilson disease / copper-overload state

## Rx
- **Dose:** 1–2 mg SC daily (or topical 1–2% serum)
- **Route:** SC or topical
- **Reconstitution:** 50 mg + 5 mL BAC water = 10 mg/mL = 10,000 mcg/mL → 2 mg = 0.20 mL = **20 units** (U-100)
- **Cycle:** 4–8 weeks
- **Stack pairs:** bpc-157, tb-500

## Labs (baseline)
- CBC, CMP
- Serum copper/ceruloplasmin if chronic systemic use
- **Age-appropriate cancer screening current ← gating**

## Ongoing care
| When | What to check | What to adjust |
|---|---|---|
| Wk 2 | Skin/site tolerance | Continue |
| Mo 1 | Response, copper if dosing high | Adjust dose/route |
| Mo 3 | Outcome, serum copper | Taper or stop |
| Mo 6 | Malignancy ROS, copper status | Stop on any red flag/overload |

## Safety & compliance gate
- ☐ Off-label/investigational consent signed (`consent_investigational_peptide`)

## Patient education key points
- Not FDA-approved as an injectable; topical use is better studied.
- Don't exceed dosing — copper can accumulate.
- Tell us any cancer history.
- Report rash, metallic taste, or unusual symptoms.
