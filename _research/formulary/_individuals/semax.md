# Semax

## Indication
Focus, cognitive support, and recovery from cognitive fatigue. <!-- NOTE: not FDA-approved; Russian-origin; native Semax SC has a short half-life (intranasal is the conventional route) — SC dosing here is empiric; N-acetyl/amidated analogs are more stable for SC -->

## Rule out (contraindications)
- ☐ Pregnancy
- ☐ Lactation
- ☐ Age <18
- ☐ Hypersensitivity
- ☐ Acute psychiatric instability (precautionary)

## Rx
- **Dose:** 300 mcg SC daily; range 250–600 mcg (divided if higher)
- **Route:** SC
- **Reconstitution:** 10 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 5 mg/mL = 5,000 mcg/mL → 300 mcg = 0.06 mL = **6 units** (U-100)
- **Cycle:** 2–4 weeks, pulsed
- **Stack pairs:** selank, dsip, cerebrolysin

## Labs (baseline)
- Generally none required; BP if hypertensive
- Psychiatric history review

## Ongoing care
| When | What to check | What to adjust |
|---|---|---|
| Wk 2 | Tolerance, focus | Continue |
| Mo 1 | Cognitive/mood effect | Hold/adjust |
| Mo 3 | Off-cycle status | Plan next pulse |
| Mo 6 | Durable benefit | Continue vs stop |

## Patient education key points
- Not FDA-approved; effects are subtle.
- Now given as a small subcutaneous injection — rotate sites.
- Report agitation, headache, or sleep changes.
- Used in short courses, not indefinitely.
