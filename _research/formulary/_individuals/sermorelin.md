# Sermorelin (Geref — discontinued)

## Indication
Adult symptomatic age-related GH decline with low-normal/low IGF-1 after excluding other causes.

## Rule out (contraindications)
- ☐ Active malignancy (absolute)
- ☐ Untreated severe hypothyroidism (treat first)
- ☐ Pregnancy
- ☐ Lactation; age <18
- ☐ Hypersensitivity
- ☐ Non-functional pituitary (won't respond)

## Rx
- **Dose:** 300 mcg SC nightly; range 200–500 mcg
- **Route:** SC, bedtime, empty stomach
- **Reconstitution:** 5 mg + 5 mL BAC water = 1 mg/mL = 1,000 mcg/mL → 300 mcg = 0.30 mL = **30 units** (U-100)
- **Cycle:** 12–24 weeks; reassess
- **Stack pairs:** ipamorelin, cjc-1295

## Labs (baseline)
- **IGF-1, TSH/free T4 ← gating** (thyroid + IGF-1)
- Fasting glucose/A1c, CMP, lipids, CBC

## Ongoing care
| When | What to check | What to adjust |
|---|---|---|
| Wk 2 | Tolerance, sleep | Reinforce timing |
| Mo 1 | Symptoms, fasting glucose | Hold dose |
| Mo 3 | IGF-1, glucose | Titrate to upper-normal IGF-1 |
| Mo 6 | IGF-1, A1c, composition | Lowest effective dose; stop if flat |

## Patient education key points
- Compounded/off-label; this is not synthetic HGH.
- Inject at bedtime on an empty stomach — no carbs first.
- Benefits are modest; we track IGF-1 and symptoms.
- Report swelling, joint aches, or tingling (signs of too much).
