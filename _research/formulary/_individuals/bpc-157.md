# BPC-157

## Indication
Adjunct for refractory soft-tissue (tendon/ligament/muscle) injury or GI mucosal complaints that have plateaued on standard care.

## Rule out (contraindications)
- ☐ Active or prior malignancy (pro-angiogenic — absolute)
- ☐ Pregnancy
- ☐ Lactation
- ☐ Age <18
- ☐ Hypersensitivity to peptide/diluent
- ☐ Undiagnosed mass / malignancy red flag

## Rx
- **Dose:** 250 mcg SC daily; range 250–500 mcg/day
- **Route:** SC (local to injury preferred)
- **Reconstitution:** 5 mg + 2 mL BAC water = 2.5 mg/mL = 2,500 mcg/mL → 250 mcg = 0.10 mL = **10 units** (U-100)
- **Cycle:** 4–6 weeks, then reassess and stop
- **Stack pairs:** tb-500, ghk-cu

## Labs (baseline)
- CBC, CMP
- **Age-appropriate cancer screening current ← gating**

## Ongoing care
| When | What to check | What to adjust |
|---|---|---|
| Wk 2 | Tolerance, injection site, response | ↑ to 500 mcg or stop if no signal |
| Mo 1 | Functional outcome (ROM/pain) | Continue short course or discontinue |
| Mo 3 | Should be off; re-exam ± image | Pivot to definitive care if unresolved |
| Mo 6 | Malignancy ROS | Stop + work up any red flag |

## Patient education key points
- Not FDA-approved; animal data only, no human efficacy proof.
- Theoretical cancer-promotion risk — tell us any cancer history.
- This supports rehab; it does not replace PT or needed surgery.
- Report any new lump, weight loss, or unusual symptom immediately.
