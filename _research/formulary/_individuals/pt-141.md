# Bremelanotide (PT-141 / Vyleesi)

## Indication
Acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women (on-label); off-label for ED or low desire.

## Rule out (contraindications)
- ☐ Uncontrolled hypertension (absolute)
- ☐ Significant cardiovascular disease
- ☐ Pregnancy
- ☐ Lactation; age <18
- ☐ Hypersensitivity
- ☐ Priapism-predisposing condition (ED use)

## Rx
- **Dose:** 1.75 mg SC PRN, ≥45 min before activity; max 1/24h, ≤8/month
- **Route:** SC PRN
- **Reconstitution:** Vyleesi autoinjector pre-mixed (1.75 mg/0.3 mL) — **no math**. Compounded: 10 mg + 5 mL = 2 mg/mL → 1.75 mg = 0.875 mL. <!-- NOTE: verify compounded concentration -->
- **Cycle:** PRN ongoing; reassess at 8 wk
- **Stack pairs:** kisspeptin-10

- Pregnancy test; reversible-cause screen (TSH, meds, mood)

## Patient education key points
- Nausea is common early — take ≥45 min before activity, with food.
- Don't use if blood pressure is uncontrolled; report chest pain.
- Repeated use can darken skin/gums (may not fully reverse).
- Episodic only — no more than 8 doses a month.
